## The constraint that shapes everything

AI makes *generation* free. It makes *coherence* expensive. The bottleneck was never author-hours ŌĆö it's keeping ~10,000 entities from contradicting each other. No context window holds the corpus, so every new entity is written against a thin slice and contradictions accumulate silently. The entire project is architected around **contradiction control**, not authoring throughput.

This is a data problem before it's a writing problem. The repo, the build pipeline, and the **edge graph are the unfair advantage** ŌĆö prose can't be checked, edges can. The graph is the canon database; tooling that polices it is load-bearing infrastructure, not a nicety.

## The generative method: the pincer

History is built from **both fixed ends inward**, so it can never float free:

- **Floor ŌåÆ up (retrodiction):** every present-day fact (a border, a ruin, a stifled trade route, a grudge) *demands a cause*, which demands an earlier cause. Drill down. Present ŌåÆ ~100 yrs prior ŌåÆ before that.
- **Ceiling ŌåÆ down:** the cosmogony's acts (God War, Hykravones, the daemons who hid and founded races) set things in motion. Drill forward.
- They **collide in the Seventh Dawn** (the current ~3,277-year age). That collision *is* the history.

The present pins it from being rootless; the myth pins it from being arbitrary. "Layers of paint that barely show" is what retrodiction naturally produces ŌĆö most history exists as *implied pressure* behind a present fact, not as foreground.

## The depth model

Depth is **referential**, not exhaustive ŌĆö even with AI. Most of the ~3,500 places stay `footnote`/`minor` *forever*; that is correct, not failure. The lever is **the gesture**: a stub that points at one higher-layer thing (a war, a migration, a dead god) reads as deep for the cost of a sentence. We flesh the nodes that history and people actually touch, and let the rest gesture upward.

## The scholar frame (contradiction absorber)

The codex reads as an in-world wiki **stitched from many scholars over time**, not an omniscient voice. ~20 loremasters: **7 majors** (each anchored to an era / theme, with a culture, a patron daemon, and a *bias*) carry the bulk; **13 niche experts** accrete. They lived on the timeline and can appear in the history they wrote.

Why it's structural, not decorative: when generation yields two conflicting facts, we **attribute the conflict** to rival scholars instead of paying to reconcile it. The hardest problem (contradiction at scale) becomes the richest texture. Lock the 7 majors early ŌĆö their voices are a coherence constraint that must not drift at entity #4,000.
