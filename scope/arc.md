# Alaria — High-Level Plan

The 20-year architecture. *What* the world needs and *how* it gets built without collapsing under its own weight. The active super-phase lives in `phase-history.md` (retired phases in `archive/`); the cross-phase ledger is `progress.md`; working rules are in `guidelines.md`.

## The constraint that shapes everything

AI makes *generation* free. It makes *coherence* expensive. The bottleneck was never author-hours — it's keeping ~10,000 entities from contradicting each other. No context window holds the corpus, so every new entity is written against a thin slice and contradictions accumulate silently. The entire project is architected around **contradiction control**, not authoring throughput.

This is a data problem before it's a writing problem. The repo, the build pipeline, and the **edge graph are the unfair advantage** — prose can't be checked, edges can. The graph is the canon database; tooling that polices it is load-bearing infrastructure, not a nicety.

## Where the world stands (updated 2026-05-28)

- **Ceiling (cosmogony):** strong. The World Timeline, the Azus/Melera duality, Ezz, the titan-gods, the three-soul death (soul→Astral, shadow→Malstaris), the daemon/Celestia faith-economy, the magic systems (Deoric / Kethic / Faesong). Real metaphysics with causal teeth.
- **Reference frames (Foundations, DONE):** the small finite frames are locked — 140 daemons over time, 13 era entities, 20 scholars with biases, ~21 onomastic sheets, a canon-linter holding `errors:0`. The Tarkhon pincer prototype proved the loop end-to-end. See `progress.md`.
- **Floor (gazetteer):** ~3,700 places from the frozen map. Half are ≤15-word stubs.
- **The missing middle (ACTIVE work):** the legendarium — the events and people connecting gods to dirt — barely exists beyond the Tarkhon set. Scaling the pincer to build it is the current super-phase (`phase-history.md`).

## The generative method: the pincer

History is built from **both fixed ends inward**, so it can never float free:

- **Floor → up (retrodiction):** every present-day fact (a border, a ruin, a stifled trade route, a grudge) *demands a cause*, which demands an earlier cause. Drill down. Present → ~100 yrs prior → before that.
- **Ceiling → down:** the cosmogony's acts (God War, Hykravones, the daemons who hid and founded races) set things in motion. Drill forward.
- They **collide in the Seventh Dawn** (the current ~3,277-year age). That collision *is* the history.

The present pins it from being rootless; the myth pins it from being arbitrary. "Layers of paint that barely show" is what retrodiction naturally produces — most history exists as *implied pressure* behind a present fact, not as foreground.

## The depth model

Depth is **referential**, not exhaustive — even with AI. Most of the ~3,500 places stay `footnote`/`minor` *forever*; that is correct, not failure. The lever is **the gesture**: a stub that points at one higher-layer thing (a war, a migration, a dead god) reads as deep for the cost of a sentence. We flesh the nodes that history and people actually touch, and let the rest gesture upward.

## The scholar frame (contradiction absorber)

The codex reads as an in-world wiki **stitched from many scholars over time**, not an omniscient voice. ~20 loremasters: **7 majors** (each anchored to an era / theme, with a culture, a patron daemon, and a *bias*) carry the bulk; **13 niche experts** accrete. They lived on the timeline and can appear in the history they wrote.

Why it's structural, not decorative: when generation yields two conflicting facts, we **attribute the conflict** to rival scholars instead of paying to reconcile it. The hardest problem (contradiction at scale) becomes the richest texture. Lock the 7 majors early — their voices are a coherence constraint that must not drift at entity #4,000.

## The work, as layers

1. **Pantheon-over-time.** Not a static list — a lifecycle. Daemons are dead mortals sustained by worship; they die when followers vanish (God War, Hykravones) and new ones rise (Tiira, 1800 SD). Old/dead, current, and rising gods. Sizing: ~50–100 cultures × 0–5 patrons with overlap ⇒ **~60–150 named daemons**, or everyone worships the same five. Gates every `worships` edge.
2. **History (the middle).** Eras as entities; the wars, migrations, falls, and race-origins as `event` entities wired with `caused` / `precededBy` / `participatedIn` / `occurredDuring`. Generated by the pincer, not by quota.
3. **People.** Founders, dynasts, rulers, legendary dead. A `person` anchors an event *and* a place at once — highest-leverage node type. Target on the order of thousands, as an *output* of the method.
4. **Gazetteer.** Tier honestly; flesh what the layers touch; every stub gets one upward gesture.

## The flip-side is an entangled second timeline

The three-soul death continuously *feeds* the flip-side: every dead soul → Astral, every shadow → Malstaris. Material history and shadow/Celestia history are **causally coupled** (Lyzaria: First Death → The First Dark). Treat Malstaris/Celestia/Labyrinth history as a **parallel track that exchanges mass with the material one**, not a side wing. Mass-exchange coupling itself has direct precedent (Glorantha's God Time/Time, Sandman's Dreaming↔waking); the three-part soul split has partial precedent (Egyptian *ren* / Pullman dæmon-ghost-spectre). What is genuinely original is the *specific synthesis*: the identity-bearer is the shortest-durability stream (ends when forgotten), and the worship/death/identity economies all route through one plane (Celestia). Lean on the Celestia configuration and the forgetting-extinction inversion — that is the load-bearing originality.

## Naming

Match name-style to race/culture (dwarf = Norse, elf = Tolkien-elf, halfling = British/medieval, …). The map names are **already drawn and frozen** — so styles are *reverse-engineered from the map*, not invented. Extract the morphemes that already exist per race/region; AI generates new names to match. Map is ground truth.
