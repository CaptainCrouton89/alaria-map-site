# Daemon economics

Invariant: Deoric spends life. Prayer is a life-tithe pooling into reserve R. R must stay above the remembrance-floor R_min (zero means forgotten, which ends the daemon) and also funds miracles (a miracle withdraws life L from R). Deficit spending below R_min kills the daemon. Model and Calibration-A numbers are both canon.

Deoric spends life. A prayer is a small life-tithe from each worshipper, pooling continuously into the daemon's reserve R. R has two obligations: it must stay above the remembrance-floor R_min (because a daemon who is forgotten ceases to exist), and it is also the fund from which miracles are paid (a miracle withdraws life L from R). Running below R_min kills the daemon. This is what the God War was: mass deficit-spending, bank-run dynamics, death-spirals.

Blood sacrifice is a lump-sum shortcut. One life yields a substantial chunk of L at once, which is why desperate cults murder instead of waiting on slow prayer accumulation.

Free-riders, favoritism, selling miracles to the rich, scale effects — a daemon with millions of followers can fund world-altering acts like ending an ice age. All of this follows from the model.

## Calibration-A canonical numbers (ratified 2026-05-27)

A typical mortal has ~40–60 max HP. The sole hard anchor: Deoric inscription = 10 HP/rune, recovered at 1 HP/week.

- **ε ≈ 0.1 HP per prayer session** (~1/400 of a mortal's max HP; negligible alone, real in aggregate).
- **R_min ≈ 50 HP-equiv** remembrance floor (~500 daily prayers: ~1.4 yr of one devotee, or 500 believers praying once).
- **Minor miracle** (cure a disease, win one battle): **L ≈ 34 HP** (~340 worshipper-prayer-days).
- **Major miracle** (save a city, one resurrection): **L ≈ 89 HP** (~890 worshipper-prayer-days).
- **World-altering** (Craggus ending the ice age): **L ≈ 50,000 HP** (~500,000 worshipper-prayer-days).
- **Blood sacrifice shortcut:** one human life ≈ **40–50 HP** ≈ 400–500 daily prayers — one murder = ~1.4 yr of one worshipper's prayers, instantly.
