# Daemon economics

Invariant: Deoric spends life. Prayer is a life-tithe pooling into reserve R. R must stay above the remembrance-floor R_min (zero means forgotten, which ends the daemon) and also funds miracles (a miracle withdraws life L from R). Deficit spending below R_min kills the daemon. Model and Calibration-A numbers are both canon.

Deoric spends life. A prayer is a small life-tithe from each worshipper, pooling continuously into the daemon's reserve R. R has two obligations: it must stay above the remembrance-floor R_min (because a daemon who is forgotten ceases to exist), and it is also the fund from which miracles are paid (a miracle withdraws life L from R). Running below R_min kills the daemon. This is what the God War was: mass deficit-spending, bank-run dynamics, death-spirals.

Blood sacrifice is a lump-sum shortcut. One life yields a substantial chunk of L at once, which is why desperate cults murder instead of waiting on slow prayer accumulation.

Free-riders, favoritism, selling miracles to the rich, scale effects — a daemon with millions of followers can fund world-altering acts like ending an ice age. All of this follows from the model.

## Calibration-A canonical numbers (ratified 2026-05-27)

A typical mortal has ~40–60 max HP. The sole hard anchor: Deoric inscription = 10 HP/rune, recovered at 1 HP/week.

- **ε ≈ 0.1 HP per prayer session** (~1/400 of a mortal's max HP; negligible alone, real in aggregate).
- **R_min ≈ 50 HP-equiv** remembrance floor (~500 daily prayers: ~1.4 yr of one devotee, or 500 believers praying once).
- **Minor miracle** (cure a disease, win one battle): **L ≈ 34 HP** (~340 worshipper-prayer-days).
- **Major miracle** (save a city, one resurrection): **L ≈ 89 HP** (~890 worshipper-prayer-days).
- **World-altering** (Craggus ending the ice age): **L ≈ 50,000 HP** (~500,000 worshipper-prayer-days).
- **Blood sacrifice shortcut:** one human life ≈ **40–50 HP** ≈ 400–500 daily prayers — one murder = ~1.4 yr of one worshipper's prayers, instantly.

## Post-mortem mastery

Daemons do not meaningfully accrue mastery after death. Psywinds-perception of Material Deoric activity adds slow, incidental polish — the daemon observes rituals conducted in its name and may internalize occasional structural patterns. This is background entropy, not a curriculum, not a path to mastery.

The structural Deoric command that elder daemons hold above current mortal practitioners is overwhelmingly pre-mortem surplus, and two things drive it. The first is the pre-burning Vyanoweir corpus: the earliest daemon-eligible scholars learned the full reconstructed grammar before the libraries burned, and that corpus is gone, leaving the living tradition to rebuild from a fraction. Daemons who internalized it carry grammar-clusters current practice has lost. The second is sheer depth of study: the oldest daemons gave entire mortal lifetimes — potentially centuries in long-lived communities — to Deoric, working from inscriptions and sources many of which are now gone entirely. Inscription freshness is a real but minor third factor. The oldest daemon read titan stone only about 200,000 years less weathered than the same stone today, a small edge on a twelve-million-year erosion curve, since every daemon-eligible mortal studied inscriptions already millions of years old.

The cap follows from deep time. The oldest possible daemon dates from the early Golden Age (~200 kya). Mastery surplus is a function of source quality, not study length after death — no daemon studied for eons, and none met a living titan.

## Knowledge as worship-currency

The chain connects directly to the model above. Structural Deoric command determines how precisely a daemon can guide a worshipper's ritual, which determines how impressive its miracles appear, which determines how many worshippers tithe ε into its reserve R. Grammar is the upstream variable in the R equation.

Teaching another daemon raises their grammar. Their improved miracles attract worshippers — and wherever worship-domains overlap, those worshippers come at the teacher's expense. R contracts. Therefore rational daemons hoard. Default behavior is secrecy. The most powerful daemons are the most secretive — their lead is structural, sharing risks parity, and parity risks reversal.

Sharing occurs only through mechanisms that neutralize or compensate the competitive risk:

- **Pantheon-cartel.** Daemons with non-overlapping worship-domains form covenants to share grammar freely within the cartel. Stability requires strict domain non-overlap: a cartel member whose domain expands into another's breaks the condition and triggers schism. Most mortal "pantheons" are cartels in daemon-economy.
- **Master-debt.** Grammar transferred in exchange for a structured worship-share tithe over N centuries, a life-tithe obligation, or a binding favor-debt. Terms are set at the student's moment of maximum vulnerability; the arrangement can be deeply asymmetric.
- **Coercion.** A more powerful daemon forces a weaker one into service, or uplifts a junior into a useful instrument. The primary window is the moment of a new daemon's ascension, when reserve R is minimal and resistance is impossible.
- **Lineage.** Deferred pending ratification. Do not author downstream entities that depend on this mechanic until the resolution document is updated.
- **Accidental leak.** Through prolonged correction-guided ritual, a worshipper may internalize grammar the daemon never consciously disclosed. Spies, apostates, and defectors carry knowledge between cults. This is the daemon-economy's structural background entropy — slow, involuntary, ineradicable.

Structural consequences follow. Daemons have economic enemies wherever domains overlap. Cartel schism follows domain expansion past non-overlap conditions. Near-R_min daemons may sell grammar in desperation, which accelerates their own collapse. A daemon's most jealously-guarded possession is pre-burning-corpus structural grammar, the early-Golden-Age grammar the living tradition has lost — exactly what Vyanoweir scholars most need.

## Lost-knowledge archive

The oldest daemons hold structural Deoric grammar the current Vyanoweir tradition has lost. This is a designed consequence of the model.

The dominant loss is the burned corpus: the pre-burning Vyanoweir libraries held a reconstructed grammar the living tradition has never recovered, and the daemons who internalized it before death hold the only complete record. Pre-mortem study also drew on inscriptions and sources now gone entirely, with the surviving stone only about 200,000 years further weathered than when the oldest daemons read it — a minor change beside the wholesale loss of the libraries. Their economic rationality keeps them from teaching what they hold.

The loss is actively maintained by daemon self-interest, not the passage of time alone. The oldest daemons know what they hold; they know its value; and the knowledge-as-worship-currency model gives them every reason to keep it. The Vyanoweir reconstruction tradition works from weathered stone and secondary sources. The gap between what scholars reconstruct and what elder daemons hold widens with every century of silence.
