# Planar stack

Invariant: 4 true planes: Material, Astral, Malstaris, Celestia. 2 overlays: Ethereal (carries astral threads), Nethereal (carries malstaric threads). These six are the canonical "six planes." Astraeva and Eindumor are presence-cores of Aurus/Nydus, not planes. Psywinds and Faesong are forces within Ezz, not planes. The 9 Elemental Planes are a separate adjacent stack reached only via leylines.

The six planes divide into four true planes and two overlays. The true planes are Material (the world), Astral (up; souls; Aurus; the White Sun), Malstaris (down; shadows; Nydus; the Black Sun), and Celestia (sideways; the mirror). The overlays are Ethereal and Nethereal, carrying astral and malstaric threads respectively through the Material world.

Malstaris is the direct symmetric counterpart to the Astral Plane, not a metaphysical oddity. Astral is up — souls, Aurus, the White Sun. Malstaris is down — shadows, Nydus, the Black Sun. They mirror each other.

The physical-slab axis and the metaphysical mirror axis are the same axis. Phlethageros is the physical underside of the Material slab, reached by descending through stone (Emphathum → Phlethageros → the Wastes). Continuing past the Wastes — off the slab's underside — deposits you in Malstaris. The old two-axis model (physical slab separate from soul/shadow mirror) is superseded.

Unified vertical stack (top to bottom): Astral / Material slab with Phlethageros as its underside / Malstaris. Malstaris is both the physical destination of continued descent and the direct metaphysical counterpart of the Astral Plane.

Celestia mirrors the Material Plane. You arrive at the mirror of wherever you crossed. It is reached sideways off any edge, not up or down.

The 9 Elemental Planes are a separate adjacent stack, not part of the six. They are reached only via leylines, which is why Kethic power feels borrowed rather than native.
