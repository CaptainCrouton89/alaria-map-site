# Technology

Invariant: Alaria's baseline is high-medieval. Steel and iron foundries, plate and crossbow and ballista, watermills and windmills, aqueducts, paved road networks, and a developed banking layer are the ordinary furniture of the world, and that baseline holds across roughly four-fifths of it. Everything that runs ahead of the baseline is a bounded exception. Each exception is fenced behind one scarce input that a single people or a single secret controls, so it produces an economic chokepoint rather than a general tier of advancement. The world once stood higher than this and fell. What runs ahead of the baseline now is a surviving fragment of that fall, not a frontier the world is climbing back up.

This is a reference frame, not a new system. It documents and bounds what the entity corpus already establishes, and it draws the line authors kept crossing. With no stated ceiling, the corpus accreted Aether engines, gunpowder, clockwork, and cyborg goblins one file at a time, each plausible alone and proto-industrial in aggregate. The ceiling is stated here so the next entity does not add a printing press or a steam railway by default.

## The baseline

A traveler crossing the inhabited world finds the same level of craft almost everywhere. Smiths work iron and steel in foundries; Ironforge runs its furnaces around the clock. Armies carry plate, crossbow, ballista, and fire-catapult. Mills turn on water and wind, fields drink from aqueducts, and goods move along paved roads and over the chokepoint tolls that tax them. Coin is lent at interest from counting-houses in Adrak and weighed against titan-bone as the reserve asset of last resort. This is the world's normal, and it does not vary much from the Middle Sea to the steppe. Nothing in it would surprise a high-medieval hand.

The exceptions are real, and they are few. They do not raise the floor. A Gruynmar firearm or an Ohblex clock-movement is a marvel precisely because the village two valleys over has never seen its like and never will.

## The lost height

Technology is capped for a historical reason, not a natural one. An older world stood higher — high enough that mortals raised works in aetherium they could not have explained, what the mage-kings called aetherial magic, which is to say Kethic elementalism channeled at a scale no one matches now. That lost-height aetherium has nothing to do with the foreign Aether the goblins burn for sky-fuel; the two are near-homophones and otherwise unrelated. That height fell. The Age of Craggus relearned fragments of it for a brief span, and the Lost Ages that followed wiped even those: the Laughing Plague burned the written record, the Blight of Arcanus turned the very fuel of aetherial work lethal for a span and toppled the empire that ran on it, and the Oblivion Years wore the memory clean out of a whole people. The ruins of that height still stand across Alaria. The knowledge to raise them again does not.

This is why the advanced pockets read as isolated and brittle rather than as the leading edge of a rising tier. Most are leavings. The Techgnomes carry broken devices and scraps of method no living gnome can fully reassemble; their masterwork turned on them and is still gutting the world from below. Most of the surviving marvels sit in the same relation to the lost age: a fragment held by one people, copied by no one, and feared more than it is envied. The foreign Aether sky-trade is the standout exception, fenced by a goblin monopoly rather than surviving as a relic of the lost height.

## The bounded exceptions

Each pocket below is kept. Each is fenced.

Aether sky-industry runs on a goblin monopoly. Aether is an exotic meta-fluid that rained down from the neighboring planar stack of Lanthornia and hangs inert and invisible in Alaria's air; it is not one of the four native sources, and it is not mundane chemistry. Only the aether-tappers can draw it from the air and fix it into fuel, a working that resembles breathing as much as craft, and attempts to replicate the process without goblin involvement have consistently failed. An Aether engine behaves like a steam engine — the struck Lanthornium drives pistons and turns propellers in a self-sustaining cycle — but the energy it releases is the discharge of an off-world planar substance, not combustion anyone can scale. It propels sky-ships and a handful of machines. It does not power a grid, a railway, or a factory, because the fuel chokes off in the hands of the clans that guard the technique. See `magic-aether.md` and `plane-astral-currents.md`.

Gruynmar gunpowder is a guarded dwarven secret. The seafaring Gruynmar clans alone understand how to make and work it, the secret dies aboard the clan-ships rather than spreading to the shore, and no other people has reproduced it. The Silzar mammoth-cannon of the plains are downstream of this monopoly, not a second source: their pieces are rationed heirlooms fed by powder they cannot make themselves. See `race-dwarf-gruynmar.md` and `race-ogre-silzar.md`.

Clockwork is secret-gated at two tiers. The Ohblex gnomes of Mkinti hold the finest — a gem-cut precision bound up with their own crystalline nature, kept inside paranoid underground houses and reproduced by no one outside the line. Camaran's export clockwork is the best human guild-craft, hand-built in limited runs under a republic that bans magic outright, a tier below the Ohblex and still nowhere near mass production. Both are fenced; neither generalizes. See `race-gnome-ohblex.md`, `mkinti.md`, and `camaran.md`.

Magic is the world's real industry, and it is fenced by the things magic needs. The Firemage Corps' military edge sits on the Yolus fire-seam under Tarkhetan and cannot be relocated; magma-channel forges, force-crystal siege weapons, and the alchemical pharma of Maurevelious all run on leylines, charged materials, and trained practitioners rather than on coal and capital. This is where Alaria concentrates the power an industrial world would draw from machines — and it is rationed by leyline geography and the cost of every source, never mass-issued. See `magic.md` and `economy.md`.

The Aciabro and Coghead goblins are a surviving fragment of the lost craft. Their augmentations are handmagic-cut and fed on Aether and blood, not bolted-on machinery, and they stay rare and secret to a single brood and a single hold. They are a relic, not a tech base. See `race-goblin-aciabro.md` and the Coghead heritage in `race-goblin.md`.

## What is out

No printing press. Knowledge copies by hand, which is why a burned library is a catastrophe the world does not recover from.

No mass production. Everything ahead of the baseline is artisan-built, one piece at a time, by the people who hold its secret.

No mass-produced or general firearms. Gunpowder does not spread beyond the Gruynmar monopoly; a musket in any other hand is contraband, loot, or a story that does not check out.

No general combustion industry. Aether engines are sky-ship propulsion fenced by goblin fuel, not a power source anyone can tap at scale — no steam railways, no powered manufactories, no engine on a frontier that the goblins do not feed.
