# Mortal economy

Invariant: Mortal commerce in Alaria runs on controlled passage, not open markets. The signature form is the chokepoint toll — a power that holds a strait, sound, or mountain pass taxes everything that must cross it. The developed core is the Middle Sea: the Aldriktch Trade Alliance caps inter-member tariffs and binds members to mutual-escort convoy; Adron is the banking-and-shipyard hub whose Adrak counting-houses underwrite Middle Sea war-loans and most sky vessels. The supreme supply-stranglehold is titan blood and bone — roughly 95% of world supply from Deo Esari, the required input for all Deoric handmagic and charged ritual. This is the mortal economy; the daemon prayer-economy in `daemon-economics.md` is the divine counterpart, governed separately.

This file is a reference frame, not a new economic system. It documents and bounds what the entity corpus already establishes. Where a lever already has its own systems doc, this file states the economic shape and defers to it; the seasons, the astral currents, the leylines, and the prayer-economy are each owned elsewhere. Money, credit, and the banking layer live in `currency.md` and are not duplicated here.

The divider that matters most is the one named in the invariant. `daemon-economics.md` covers how gods are funded: prayer is a life-tithe pooling into a daemon's reserve, miracles are paid out of it, and knowledge is hoarded as a competitive asset. None of that is coin, cargo, or toll. This file covers the other thing entirely — how mortals move goods and who taxes them on the way.

## The chokepoint-toll model

Alaria's distinctive economic form is not the market or the guild. It is the gate. Geography pinches trade through a handful of straits, sounds, and passes, and whoever holds one of those narrows charges for the crossing. The wealth follows the chokehold, not the goods.

The longest example is the corridor running from the Middle Sea to the Shattered Sea, where a single westbound cargo pays three separate powers in sequence. The first toll is the Tarkhon Strait, the only sea door out of the inner sea, where Tarkhetan's navy and fire-mage garrison make payment a fact rather than a request — a hull pays going out and pays again coming home, and the empire takes the largest cut because it holds the one gate the others cannot open. Past the narrows the route crosses the Sea of Merchants to Sheîr, which sells the pilots, the charts, and the safe approach through its eastern islands and charges for all three. North of there the road runs into Phyndarr Sound, where Tollgate takes the last toll at the Sound's mouth. None of the three coordinates with the others. Each simply holds its own door.

The same form repeats at smaller scale. Inside the Middle Sea, the Telphineas Strait is the one deepwater channel joining the eastern and western halves, and Watar and Bestacia are sliding toward open quarrel over it — Watar holds the northwest shore and has begun levying an anchorage fee the Aldriktch charter never permitted, while Bestacia's claim runs through the Alliance courts because a wall of mountains keeps it off the water entirely. To the north of Tarkhon, the Vogenfeld passes through the Wurmspine are the only army-route between the orcish hordes and the imperial heartland, and the Uline dwarves who hold them are a security stranglehold the whole toll-rich coast depends on without ever having paid for. Kazül, the last harbor before the Tarkhon narrows, makes the model pay in reverse: it sits close enough to the chokepoint to skim the traffic that would rather not be counted at the toll-houses, and the smuggling around the empire's count is one of the kingdom's steadier trades.

## The Middle Sea trade architecture

The Middle Sea is the corpus's most developed trade region, and its economy is built around the same chokepoint pressure the corridor exerts from outside. The states there are small and, several of them, militarily negligible. Cooperation is how they survive the corsairs and the toll wars.

The Aldriktch Trade Alliance is the instrument. Six members signed the charter at the free harbor of Aldriktch on the Watari coast: Erasnus, Watar, Myorna, Camaran, Bestacia, and Ubrik. It caps the tariffs members may charge one another and obliges them to escort each other's shipping, so that raiders face a sea too well-watched to work. For two centuries it held. It is straining now, against Myorna's contested admission, the silvertongue collapse in Ubrik, Camaran's inward turn, and the Watar-Bestacia quarrel over the Telphineas Strait. Erasnus, which owns no fleet and no fields but every other nation's need to move goods across its roads, profits most from the pact and has the most to lose if it comes apart.

Adron is the financial center. The eastern Naga kingdom is distant from the Tarkhon chokepoint, and that distance is exactly what lets it play financier to the states that sit nearer the throat — it lends at interest from the depth of its Adrak counting-houses, and when a Middle Sea war needs funding the loan is usually written in Adrak. Adron also builds and finances the hulls: a fair share of the bottoms carrying Camaran's manufactures and the eastern harvests around the rim are Adron-built and Adron-owned, and its banks underwrite most of the continent's sky vessels as well. Camaran is the workshop opposite it, a republic turning out tools, textiles, clockwork, and precision instruments under anti-magic statutes that bar its own workshops from growing the desert glass it buys from Kazül in trade.

Grain has its own institution, and it sits outside the Alliance. The Tarkhon Empire sets the price of its bread each year at the Grain Exchange in Aldrichold, where licensed imperial merchants grade the crop and name its worth — the arrangement that binds the farming kingdom of Enymu to supply Tarkhetan's bread at a price the empire decides. It is the most mechanically specific economic institution in the corpus and a monopsony in everything but the word, distinct from the Aldriktch tariff regime despite the similar name.

## The commodity map

A handful of materials carry economic weight out of all proportion to their bulk, and most of them are tied to a single source that cannot be moved.

Titan blood and bone is the supreme stranglehold. It is the only material that holds Deoric charge durably, which makes it the required input for all handmagic tattooing, Deoric ink, and charged ritual — no mortal blood, alchemical substitute, or synthetic compound replaces it. Roughly 95% of the world's supply comes from Deo Esari, the insular mountain kingdom whose Titanic Priesthood guards the bones as sacred trust. The monopoly is tightening not from competition but from depletion: veins run dry, a contamination disaster two centuries ago cut harvesting capacity by some 40%, and prices climb year over year. Where the price spikes, the murders follow, because every handmagic user carries valuable material in their tattooed skin.

Red gold is Adron's, mined from its peaks. It never tarnishes and turns aside elemental Kethic the way it turns aside light, which sets it among the magical materials rather than the merely precious ones; it is rare enough that most of what circulates is recut from older work, and a single recovered shield can change hands for the price of a small estate.

Aether is the fuel of the sky trade, and it is a goblin monopoly. The meta-fluid arrived from the planar stack Lanthornia and hangs invisible in the air, and only the aether-tapper goblins can extract and process it — attempts to replicate the work without goblin involvement have consistently failed, which puts a chokehold on the energy source of an entire transport network in the hands of the clans that guard the technique. Sky-stone is the other half of the sky economy: iridescent astral rock that floats in the Material atmosphere and provides the lift a sky ship hangs from, an investment so large that most stones are owned by merchant houses and state navies rather than captains.

The rest of the map is geographic and unglamorous. Desert glass comes from Kazül, worked better there than anywhere in Alaria and shipped east to Camaran. Silver comes from the Grey Mountains, the basis of Myorna's late and uneasy admission to the Alliance. Diamonds and gem-work come from Mkinti, the Ohblex gnome kingdom that trades its clockwork and stones north through Lier for agricultural goods and access to the broader markets it will not enter directly.

## Pointers to the owning systems

Three of Alaria's distinctive economic levers are built on cosmological systems with their own detail files. This frame states the economic shape and defers; do not duplicate the mechanics here.

The sun-tax economy belongs to the seasons. Because Bryn is a spotlight-sun sung onto its trail by prayer-coalitions, warmth itself is taxable: the ecclesiastical bodies that maintain the approved singing rotation levy a tithe on the communities in Bryn's path, enforced by a law of light that criminalizes unauthorized coalitions, and a community that refuses to pay loses its warmth-season the following quarter. The full apparatus and its treaty foundation are in `seasons.md`, `calendar.md`, and `event-solar-accord.md`.

The sky-trade economy belongs to the astral currents. Trade rides invisible highways where the Astral Sea's currents dip near the Material surface, on ships lifted by sky-stone and steered by Aether engines, and the wealth concentrates at the convergence points where currents overlap and at the docking towers that tax every mooring. The slave economies of Gorath and Kyagos are built on it. The currents, the hubs, the aether-engine mechanism, and the cost structure that keeps regions off the currents poor are all in `plane-astral-currents.md`.

The leyline-lease economy belongs to the leylines and is best seen at Besnoumeru, where the Izzus time-seam surfaces closer to the material world than almost anywhere else, close enough that a trained shaper can buy a few seconds' head start on the trading floor. A handful of older merchant houses hold the ground where the seam runs shallowest and lease the stretched moment, ruining any house that works it without paying — and the empire cannot tax it, because the seam answers to a shaper's feeling and feeling is not a thing a tax-collector can seize. See `besnoumeru-2561.md` and `magic-time.md`.

Money, credit, and the banking layer have their own reference file. The chokepoint tolls and commodity trades described here are denominated, settled, and financed through a system documented in `currency.md` — the loose metal standard, the de-facto reserve assets, and the credit institutions that turn Adron's counting-houses, the contract-daemon notaries, and the old banking houses into a working financial layer. This file does not restate it.
