# Seasons

Invariant: No axial tilt. Bryn is a spotlight-sun; day is Bryn overhead, night is Bryn over the far side, with no under-slab travel. Congregations sing Bryn onto north or south trails. The regular ~200-day calendar is a recent political treaty (the Solar Accord), not physics; seasons are variable-length. Poles are cold because the sun's band never reaches them.

Bryn is an awakened sun, not a star on a fixed orbit. It chooses a path; congregations sing it onto north or south trails, shifting the band of warmth accordingly. There is no axial tilt.

The Solar Accord — the treaty that standardized a roughly 200-day calendar — is recent and political, not physical. Before it and outside of it, seasons last as long as the prevailing prayer-coalition can hold Bryn on a given path.

The poles are cold because Bryn's band never reaches them, not because of tilt or distance.

The history implied by this system is canon: the far north and far south once had sun, lost it as prayer-coalitions shifted, and now hold the ruins of those sunlit eras. Relict wildlife stranded in frozen latitudes. Sun-wars fought to seize coalitions and drag Bryn's path. Solar theocracy, sun-tax, the law of light. The "let Bryn rest" heretical sect.
