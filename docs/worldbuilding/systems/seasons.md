# Seasons

Invariant: No axial tilt. Bryn is a spotlight-sun; day is Bryn overhead, night is Bryn over the far side, with no under-slab travel. Congregations move Bryn onto north or south trails by singing Faesong — Melera's harmony-aspect of Ezz, which the awakened sun hears and answers. This is not daemon prayer, and Bryn is not a daemon. The regular ~200-day calendar is a recent political treaty (the Solar Accord), not physics; seasons are variable-length. Poles are cold because the sun's band never reaches them. Two temperature drivers run at once: where Bryn's warmth-band falls (political, and quick to shift) and the slow drift of Aurus and Nydus (astronomical, the baseline beneath it).

## Bryn, the awakened sun

The sun is not a star on a fixed orbit. It is the largest of the titan-crafted stars, and when the Ezz Rift flooded Alaria it woke — gained a spark of spirit, a will, the capacity to want. That awakened spirit is Bryn. The sun travels because Bryn chooses to travel, and Bryn's chosen path can be moved. There is no axial tilt; the full account of Bryn's nature lives in `person-bryn.md`.

## Singing the sun: Faesong, not prayer

Congregations move Bryn by singing. The mechanism is Faesong — the harmony-aspect of Ezz, Melera's own ambient music, one of the four magic sources. A congregation that sings in harmony works Faesong into the world, and Bryn, itself an Ezz-woken spirit born of Melera's flooding music, hears it and answers by shifting its trail. When the northern monasteries sing loudest, Bryn carries the warmth-band north; when the southern congregations raise their voices, the band travels south.

This is the distinction that keeps the system coherent, and `person-bryn.md` states it plainly: the singing is not worship in the daemon sense. A daemon is sustained by a life-tithe of prayer that pools into its reserve and pays for its miracles, carried across the planar boundary on the Psywinds. Bryn has no such reserve, takes no tithe, and grants no miracles. Bryn is not a daemon and existed long before Celestia bloomed. The congregations are not feeding a god; they are making music a listening sun decides to follow. The two practices look alike from outside — people gathered, voices raised toward the sky — and they run on entirely different machinery. Faesong moves Bryn. Prayer feeds daemons.

## The Solar Accord and the variable year

The Solar Accord — the treaty that standardized a roughly 200-day calendar — is recent and political, not physical. Before it and outside it, seasons last as long as the prevailing coalition can hold Bryn on a given trail: ninety days for a strong coalition, forty for a collapsing one. The Accord partitioned Bryn's annual path into eight quarters, four warm and four cool, and bound the signatory coalitions to a rotation. It standardized the plan of the year. It did not change how Bryn works, so seasons remain variable in practice — a coalition that defects, collapses, or loses a sun-war extends or cuts short a quarter without warning. The full structure of the civil year is in `calendar.md`; the named quarters and holidays are in `plane-time-and-seasons.md`.

## Two temperatures

Heat in Alaria has two separate causes, and confusing them is easy because both produce warm years and cold ones.

The first is political and quick. Bryn's warmth-band falls where the singers put it. A region in the band has its summer; a region the band has left goes cold within a quarter. This is the temperature the sun-tax is levied on and the sun-wars are fought over, and it can change hands in a single season.

The second is astronomical and slow. Aurus, the White Sun above the Astral Plane, and Nydus, the Black Sun above Malstaris, drift toward and away from the Material boundary across the long turn of the Great Cycle. When Aurus draws near, warmth bleeds across the whole world regardless of where Bryn walks — the period called Faesummer, when light-creatures of the Astral spill out across a more habitable land. When Nydus draws near, cold spikes everywhere at once, the Shadowrift periods. No coalition causes Faesummer and no monastery can sing it away; it is the baseline the political band rides on top of. A warm Faesummer century forgives a coalition that loses a few quarters. A Shadowrift makes even a held warmth-band thin.

## The sunless reaches

The far north and far south are cold by default, because Bryn's band cannot be steered far enough from the equatorial belt to reach them for long. They were not always cold. Before the Accord constrained the path, competing coalitions sometimes pulled Bryn toward the poles, and warmth held in the deep north for generations before the coalition that held it collapsed. The ruins standing in those glaciers are cities built in the sunlit centuries. The relict wildlife stranded there outlived the warmth that bred it. The two regions carry their own entries: `region-sun-forsaken-north.md` and `region-sun-forsaken-south.md`.

The history this implies is canon: sun-wars fought to seize coalitions and drag Bryn's path; a solar theocracy; the sun-tax; the law of light; and the "let Bryn rest" heretical sect.
