# World-systems invariants reference

## How to use this reference

This document is the binding canon for Alaria's world-systems. No entity may contradict anything in the hard-rule table below. The design log at `docs/worldbuilding/cosmology-decisions.md` is the record of *why* decisions were made — cite it for rationale, but consult this file for authority.

If your entity touches a thread listed under "do not author yet": state only what is settled in the entity, flag the open part in an `<!-- author-notes -->` block, and defer. Do not invent resolutions.

---

## The spine

Everything traces back here. Azus is order, thought, structure, and specificity. His language is Deoric — a command tongue that reshapes reality and costs life. Melera is chaos, emotion, beauty, and harmony. Her output is Faesong, ambient music that pervades existence. Together they made Ezz, the universal substrate: equal parts thought and emotion.

Melera is imprisoned. Her escaped ripple of music caused the Ezz Rift 12 million years ago, flooding Alaria with Ezz. That flood birthed spirit and emotion as forces in the world, woke Bryn, drove the titans to madness, and caused Celestia to bloom. Every claim about magic, planes, or death must trace cleanly back to this.

## Hard-rule table

The rows below are non-negotiable. An entity that contradicts any of them is wrong. Each system links to its per-system detail file in `docs/worldbuilding/systems/`.

| System | Invariant |
|--------|-----------|
| [Planes](systems/planar-stack.md) | 4 true planes: Material, Astral, Malstaris, Celestia. 2 overlays: Ethereal (carries astral threads), Nethereal (carries malstaric threads). These six are the canonical "six planes." Astraeva and Eindumor are presence-cores of Aurus/Nydus, not planes. Psywinds and Faesong are forces within Ezz, not planes. The 9 Elemental Planes are a separate adjacent stack reached only via leylines. Unified vertical stack: Astral (up) / Material slab + Phlethageros (middle) / Malstaris (bottom, directly below the Wastes). Malstaris is reachable by physical descent past the Wastes; the "two-axis" model is superseded. |
| [Magic](systems/magic.md) | Four sources: Deoric (Azus; costs life), Psywinds (Ezz's thought/intention aspect), Faesong (Melera's emotion/harmony aspect of Ezz), Kethic (the caster's own emotion structured through the Elemental Planes via leylines). Psywinds and Faesong are two faces of the same Ezz — thought vs emotion — with no scholarly ambiguity. |
| [Seasons](systems/seasons.md) | No axial tilt. Bryn is a spotlight-sun; day is Bryn overhead, night is Bryn over the far side, with no under-slab travel. Congregations move Bryn onto north or south trails by singing Faesong (Melera's harmony-aspect of Ezz, which the awakened sun hears and answers) — this is not daemon prayer, and Bryn is not a daemon. The regular ~200-day calendar is a recent political treaty (the Solar Accord), not physics; seasons are variable-length. Two temperature drivers act at once: where Bryn's warmth-band falls (political, fast) and the slow Aurus/Nydus drift (astronomical baseline — Faesummer warm, Shadowrift cold). Poles are cold because the sun's band never reaches them. |
| [Daemons](systems/daemon-economics.md) | Deoric spends life. Prayer is a life-tithe pooling into reserve R. R must stay above the remembrance-floor R_min (zero means forgotten, which ends the daemon) and also funds miracles (a miracle withdraws life L from R). Deficit spending below R_min kills the daemon. Model and Calibration-A numbers are both canon — see `systems/daemon-economics.md` for the full scale (ε ≈ 0.1 HP/prayer; R_min ≈ 50 HP-equiv; minor miracle ≈ 34 HP; major ≈ 89 HP; world-altering ≈ 50,000 HP; blood sacrifice ≈ 40–50 HP/life). |
| [Calendar](systems/calendar.md) | SD/BSD is the sole civil calendar and dates all history; SD counts forward from calendar zero (0 SD, fixed just after the World Fire of ~10 BSD) and BSD backward from it. Now = 3376 SD. Seventh Dawn is the current era. The Great Cycle (its halves marked F and B; 25,300 yr; Aurus/Nydus drift and the lunar alignment it produces) is a cyclical astronomical position, observed and never used to date events — it has no year zero and does not convert to civil dates. Deep-prehistory is dated in approximate years-ago; from the Lost Ages onward the BSD/SD record is precise (years-ago = BSD + 3,376), placing the Shattering at ~13,500 years ago (10,209 BSD). |
| [Economy](systems/economy.md) | Mortal commerce runs on controlled passage, not open markets. The signature form is the chokepoint toll: a power holding a strait, sound, or mountain pass taxes everything that must cross it (the Tarkhon Strait → Sheîr → Tollgate corridor from the Middle Sea to the Shattered Sea; the internal Telphineas Strait; the Vogenfeld passes). The developed core is the Middle Sea — the Aldriktch Trade Alliance caps inter-member tariffs and binds members to mutual-escort convoy; Adron is the banking-and-shipyard hub whose Adrak counting-houses underwrite Middle Sea war-loans and most sky vessels. The supreme supply-stranglehold is titan blood and bone: ~95% of world supply from Deo Esari, the required input for all Deoric handmagic and charged ritual. This is the MORTAL economy; the daemon prayer-economy (`systems/daemon-economics.md`) is the DIVINE counterpart, governed separately. Money/credit/currency detail lives in `systems/currency.md`. |
| [Technology](systems/technology.md) | Baseline is high-medieval (steel/iron foundries, plate/crossbow/ballista, watermills/windmills, aqueducts, road networks, banking) across ~80% of the world. Everything ahead of baseline is a bounded exception fenced behind one scarce monopoly input — an economic chokepoint, not a general tier: aether sky-industry (goblin-extraction monopoly; replication without goblins "has consistently failed"); Gruynmar gunpowder (guarded clan secret that does not spread — Silzar mammoth-cannon are downstream consumers, not a 2nd source); Ohblex/Camaran clockwork (racial-secret + guild-gated precision, hand-built); force-crystal and leyline magic-as-tech (the world's real "industry," fenced by un-relocatable leylines and per-source cost); Aciabro/Coghead fleshcraft and Techgnome relics (surviving fragments of a lost craft). The cap is historical: an older aetherial (Kethic) industrial high-water mark was lost across the Lost Ages (burned, poisoned in the Blight of Arcanus, forgotten in the Oblivion Years) and never recovered. HARD OUT: no printing press; no mass production; no mass-produced or general firearms; gunpowder does not spread beyond the Gruynmar monopoly; no general combustion industry (aether engines are sky-ship propulsion, not a power grid). Aether is foreign (from the Lanthornia stack), exotic, and is NOT a fifth native magic source — the four-source Magic row is unaffected. |
| [Currency](systems/currency.md) | No single currency; regional standards are deliberate. Middle Sea core (the six Aldriktch Trade Alliance members + the Tarkhon Empire) reckons in the Aldriktch standard: a loose IRON-denominated token coinage minted/underwritten by the Adron banking houses (Royal Adrak Bank), climbing iron piece (1) → obol (10, bronze) → lire (100, silver) → ducat (1,000, gold-bearing) → crown (10,000, platinum-bearing) → Crimson Crown (red platinum, above crown, near-uncirculated). The south (Kyagos, Aboyinzu, the South-Rimihuica Shacklands, the Upoceax Free Isles) runs a genuinely distinct standard: weighed fine silver (the "mark"), no central mint. The Dalizi Confederation keeps no coin and reckons in cattle (ng'ombe). Both metal standards peg to one number only because the Royal Adrak Bank publishes the silver↔iron cross-rate ("Adron rates"). Above all coin: titan bone is the reserve of last resort; red gold is the supra-luxury store of value. Red platinum (the Crimson Crown) is a DISTINCT element from red gold — it does NOT turn Kethic, and that difference is the canonical counterfeit-test. Money/credit detail in systems/currency.md; goods/tolls in systems/economy.md; the prayer-economy (NOT money) in systems/daemon-economics.md. |
| [Death](systems/first-death.md) | Three strands: soul → Astral (cycles through Aurus), shadow → Malstaris (cycles through Nydus), spirit → Celestia (lasts only while the true name is remembered; forgotten means it ends permanently). |
| [Celestia](systems/celestia-return.md) | Celestia is the Material Plane's mirror flip-side, reached sideways across any edge. Return from Celestia requires that a soul or shadow anchor was preserved on the material side. The God War destroyed most anchors, trapping those who had crossed without one. |
| [Races](systems/races.md) | Interbreeding follows origin-substance. Gaea-flesh family (humans, beastmen, giants, trolls) interbreed; half-giants and trollkin exist. Druid-crafted elves (wood, stone, river-mud) cannot cross with flesh; no half-elves. Daemon-descended lines follow their own rules. Beastmen are Gaea's minor animal-children. The sacred three — two sons and a daughter — are Wolf, Lion, and Dragon: the wolf-mother Ulvma, the lion-father Shara Bolasi, and the dragon-father Nagatayora (→ Ulvsjael / Sharabha / Naga). Gaea's dormancy is the cumulative self-expenditure of Kethic across ~12 My of creation: psyic energy spent through Kethic diffused into the Ezz via Psywinds and did not return — she is diffused into her own creations, not worship-starved. The Gaea entity (`person-gaea.md`) is fully authored. |
| [Deep time / stasis](systems/races.md) | Species are Deoric-defined and do not biologically drift (true name = definition). Canonical trimmed deep-prehistory dates: Age of Titans 23.5 Mya; Ezz Rift / Gaea born 12 Mya; Reign of Dragons 10–2.5 Mya; Walk of Elves 2.5 Mya; Eyachria vs Gaea 600,000 ya; Birth of Man 500,000 ya; Golden Age onward unchanged. See `systems/races.md` for the full trim table. |
| [Celest-origin](../cosmology-decisions.md) | All three titan-gods (Aurus, Nydus, Celest) began as genuine individuated beings. Aurus and Nydus diffused — shedding selfhood into the impersonal forces they embody. Celest uniquely did NOT diffuse: she preserved a dormant, identity-bearing selfhood sealed in Celestia's core (present without will, ancient without voice — the core of a plane, not the plane itself). The titan-god / titan distinction is preserved. Celest's core is sealed and silent: it cannot reach Celestia's surface across the insulating Ezz and cannot transmit Deoric — Celest is NOT a Deoric source. The Celestia seed was an empty latent vessel for its first 11.5 My (the "refuge for the dead" framing is a retrospective mortal reading); the Ezz flood condensed into it because it was the only Deorically-shaped non-corporeal vessel in Alaria. See `cosmology-decisions.md §10`. |
| [Deoric transmission](systems/magic.md) | Deoric is the titans' native language. The titans inscribed it on stone, bone, and varied media; titan-bone inscriptions do not decay (bone invariant); stone and organic inscriptions weathered and were mostly lost over 12+ million years. Mortal Deoric is a fragmentary reconstruction — the Vyanoweir civilization led this reconstruction; their burned libraries were a catastrophic setback. Daemons are supreme practitioners through the now-destroyed pre-burning Vyanoweir corpus and a mortal lifetime's depth of pre-mortem study; the oldest died as long as ~200,000 years ago (a time-depth, not a 200ky-long study) and read titan stone only ~200ky less weathered than today's — a minor edge, never any "freshest state." They are not the language's originators or transmitters. For functional handmagic and charged ritual work, titan blood/bone remains required (only titan materials hold Deoric charge durably). See `cosmology-decisions.md §9` (base model) and `§11` (corpus + depth-of-study / ≤200ky time-depth tuning). |
| [Daemon substrate gate](../cosmology-decisions.md) | Daemon-eligibility requires that the spirit-substrate be Ezz-spirit — a mortal who died carrying Ezz. Malstaris entities (Stalactites-of-Geth class and all Malstaris demons), titans themselves, divine forces, and dragons are NOT daemon-eligible regardless of their command of Deoric or any other qualification. The substrate gate, the knowledge gate, and the worship gate (R above R_min; see Daemons row) are three independent necessary conditions; satisfying one does not relax the others. See `cosmology-decisions.md §11`. |
| [Daemon knowledge gate](../cosmology-decisions.md) | Daemon-eligibility requires grammar-internalized command of Deoric held IN LIFE — a phase transition from template-execution (artifact-keys, handmagic gesture-triggers, issued imperatives) to generative grammar (compose novel commands, nest and condition structures, scope effects dynamically, detect grammar flaws in unfamiliar workings). This threshold is categorical, not a gradient. Template-level practitioners — including Veleth Korrandus and all handmagicians — are below it regardless of power or scale. The Vyanoweir reconstruction tradition is the first and foundational mortal path to crossing this threshold. See `cosmology-decisions.md §11`. |
| [First daemon era](../cosmology-decisions.md) | The Golden Age of Man is the first era in which any daemon ascended; there are no pre-Vyanoweir daemons. The 11.8-million-year silence between the Ezz Rift and the first Golden-Age daemon (~200 kya) is a graveyard of Ezz-spirits who never reached the knowledge gate — most held no Deoric at all; a handful held template-level fragments; all faded as they were forgotten. No dragon, elf, peri-Rift mortal, or alternative grammar tradition produced a daemon before the Vyanoweir reconstruction. See `cosmology-decisions.md §11`. |
| [Daemon knowledge hoarding](../cosmology-decisions.md) | Daemons hoard structural Deoric grammar by default — sharing raises a competitor's miracle quality, threatens worshipper-base overlap, and contracts R. Sharing occurs only through five mechanisms: pantheon-cartel (non-overlapping domain covenant; cartel members share grammar freely because domain non-overlap prevents worship competition); master-debt (structured tithe or obligation in exchange for teaching); coercion (forced uplift or binding at ascension, when the new daemon's R is minimal); lineage (parent→child loyalty where shared worship pools align incentives — this mechanic is deferred, do not author downstream entities that depend on it); accidental leak (worshipper internalizes grammar through prolonged correction-guided ritual; mortal-channel slow background entropy). The oldest daemons hold structural grammar the current Vyanoweir tradition has lost; economic rationality keeps them from teaching it. See `cosmology-decisions.md §11`. |

Per-system detail — extended prose, inline open flags, and worked examples — lives in `docs/worldbuilding/systems/`. Each table link leads directly to the relevant file.
