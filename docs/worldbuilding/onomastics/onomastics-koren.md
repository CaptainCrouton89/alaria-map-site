# Koren Onomastics

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Author anchor:** Extremely short and hard — 1–2 syllables, minimal vowels  
**Source pool (frozen map names — do not copy verbatim):** Kor, Korel

## Sound palette
- Consonants that recur: K/r as the defining consonant pair; hard initial K; terminal -r or -rel (liquid close)
- Vowel behavior: single short o as the only attested vowel; monosyllabic (Kor) or bisyllabic (Korel); no diphthongs, no open vowels, no trailing vowels
- Forbidden / off-style: anything over 3 syllables (reads Kendor or elven), flowing vowel chains, soft openers — Koren names are the opposite of Kendor

## Morphemes & affixes
- Root: **Kor-** — the race name is the root; all attested names share it
- Terminals: silent (Kor), -el (Korel = a liquid softening of the bare root)
- **FLAG:** two samples, both sharing the same root — impossible to distinguish whether Kor- is a phoneme pattern universal to the race or whether both samples happen to be the same stem

## Naming customs
- Brevity is the style — a Koren name should feel cut short compared to any other race's name
- The Kor- root's ubiquity may mean it's a racial/clan prefix (all Koren carry it) rather than coincidence
- Until pool grows, default to Kor- + short terminal: Kor, Korel, and analogues
