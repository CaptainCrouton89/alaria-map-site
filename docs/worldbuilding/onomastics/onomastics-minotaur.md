## Sound palette
- Consonants that recur: single hard consonant per syllable (d/n/k, l/r/n, r/n/t); no clusters; terminals are hard stops or sonorants (-ki, -ean, -tar)
- Vowel behavior: open vowels (a, o, e) predominate; 2–3 syllables; Romance-adjacent without being Italian
- Forbidden / off-style: consonant clusters (reads dwarf/orc), Ix- prefix (reads lizardfolk), -ling (reads halfling/goblin), anything over 4 syllables

## Morphemes & affixes
- No clear prefix pattern from 3 samples
- Terminals: -aki/-ki (Danaki), -ean/-an (Lorean), -tar (Rentar) — hard or sonorant closing
- Syllable structure: CV-CV-CV or CV-CVC (two to three open syllables)

## Naming customs
- Short, powerful — minotaur names carry weight through brevity and hard terminals, not through consonant stacking
- The three samples suggest personal names; place/clan names likely follow the same short form
- Do not extend these to 4+ syllables without more corpus support
