# Tuktuk Onomastics

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Author anchor:** Two sub-registers — reduplicative martial (personal names) and runic-vowel-shifted (place names)  
**Source pool (frozen — do not copy verbatim):** Tuktuk (race name), Tyvern (home island)

## Sound palette
- **Tuktuk**: Tuk + tuk — exact reduplication; T-opener, short u vowel, hard k terminal; percussive and martial
- **Tyvern**: Ty + vern — Ty- opener (t+y glide), v medial, -ern terminal (northern/runic close)
- Two distinct sub-registers that are recognizably from the same culture:
  1. **Martial / personal**: reduplication of T+short vowel+k syllable; punchy and memorable in battle
  2. **Place names**: Ty- opener + consonant bridge + -ern/-orn/-arn terminal
- Forbidden / off-style (personal): soft or open-vowel endings; names longer than 2 syllables for personal register; anything that can't be bellowed
- Forbidden / off-style (place): reduplication; soft openers; long vowel sequences

## Morphemes & affixes
- Martial register: **T-** or **K-** opener + short vowel (u, a) + hard k terminal; full reduplication of the syllable is the strongest form
- Place register: **Ty-/T-** opener + **-v-** or other consonant bridge + **-ern/-orn/-arn** terminal
- The hard-k sound (Tuktuk) and the ty/vy consonant texture (Tyvern) are the two anchors
- Reduplication in the personal register signals martial standing; a non-reduplicated name reads as a child-name or outsider-given name

## Naming customs
- Warrior personal names use reduplication (Tuktuk = "double-Tuk") or a compressed single T/K form (Tuk as a short name)
- Clan names likely follow the reduplication pattern extended: three-beat forms possible for legendary clans
- Place names follow Tyvern's structure: Ty-/T- + consonant interior + -ern/-orn type terminal; the volcanic island culture links place-naming to rugged, nasal-closed sounds
- The two sub-registers are not mixed: a Tuktuk warrior's given name would not follow the Tyvern-type pattern and vice versa

## Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Torgvern** — place (volcanic district): Tor- (T + or, vowel variant of Ty-) + -gvern (-g+vern, heavier consonant variant of -vern); a forge district or sea-facing strongpoint
- **Kuvtuk** — personal (warrior): Kuv- (k + uv, hard-k opener with v-bridge before) + -tuk (reduplication-half as terminal); a given name
- **Tykarn** — place (coastal fortification): Tyk- (cf. Tyvern's Ty- + k) + -arn (-arn, -ern terminal with vowel shift); a sea wall or harbor name
