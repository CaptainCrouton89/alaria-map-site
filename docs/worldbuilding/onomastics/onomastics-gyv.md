# Gyv Onomastics

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Author anchor:** Compressed personal forms (v-terminal), vowel-opened polity names (-ia close) — smooth, unobtrusive, v-dominant  
**Source pool (frozen — do not copy verbatim):** Gyv (race name), Iylovia (polity)

## Sound palette
- **Gyv**: G + y + v — single syllable; g-opener, y-vowel glide, v-terminal; compressed near-sigil
- **Iylovia**: Iy + lo + vi + a — doubled vowel opener (iy-), liquid l interior, v medial, -ia terminal
- V-sound: the defining phoneme — terminal in Gyv, medial in Iylovia; present in every confirmed sample
- Vowel behavior: i/y vowels dominant (Gyv's y-glide, Iylovia's iy-); -ia terminal marks polity/place names
- Consonants: soft — g, l, v; no hard clusters; the culture of undetected influence is reflected in names that are smooth and unobtrusive
- Forbidden / off-style: percussive hard stops or clusters; reduplication (that reads as goblin/tuktuk); guttural openers; anything that announces itself

## Morphemes & affixes
- G-/Gy- opener: race name; personal names start here
- V-sound (medial or terminal): **universal** — appears in both samples; the one obligatory phoneme in any Gyv name
- Iy-/I- vowel opener: Iylovia — marks formal polity and place names; not used for personal names (those compress to G-)
- -ia terminal: Iylovia — polity/place terminal; personal names use harder compressed terminals (-v, -l, -yl)
- Short personal forms: 1–2 syllables with v-sound; longer forms (3–4 syllables) for place/polity names only

## Naming customs
- Personal names are compressed — often 1–2 syllables, always with v-sound; the culture prizes unobtrusive influence, and names that are easily forgotten serve that
- Polity/place names follow Iylovia's pattern: Iy-/I- double-vowel opener + liquid interior + v medial + -ia terminal
- The v-sound is the non-negotiable Gyv identity marker; a name without it reads as outsider or alias
- The Gyv's half-Zaelian heritage might produce hybrid names, but pure Gyv register always anchors on the v-sound

## Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Gyval** — personal: Gyv- (race-name stem) + -al (-al open terminal softening the v-close); a given name
- **Iyvonia** — place: Iyo- (cf. Iy- in Iylovia, vowel variant) + -vonia (v + onia, extending Iylovia's -lovia with heavier interior); a Gyv city name
- **Velgyi** — personal: Vel- (v + el, v-first variant) + -gyi (g + yi, Gyv-stem inverted as close); a name that blends both morpheme families
