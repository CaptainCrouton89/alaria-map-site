# Human Onomastics

**Author anchor:** Maximum regional variation by design — document as families, do NOT blend  
**Source pool (frozen map names — do not copy verbatim):** Bledreon, Drachma, Gezzerin, Husakas, Inavolin, Kuru, Neth, Nuun, Oznak, Qindo, Qord'ik, Shapers, Shontobi, Shyona, Stone Men, Tarnak, Temptari, The Guiltless, Tikhaya, Vyanoweir, Wendi — place names: Atyeska, Avakus Forest, Wurn Mountains, Ulkevolm, Plains of Oblivion, Sheîr, Free Isles, Hills of Gezzeri

> **Rule: pick a regional family and stay in it. Do NOT blend families into a single name.**  
> A human name that looks partly Romance and partly invented-harsh is a coherence violation. Choose one family for the culture/character/place you are naming.

---

## Family 1 — Romance (Mediterranean/Italian-adjacent)

**Pool samples:** Drachma, Gezzerin, Temptari, Inavolin, Kuru

- Consonants: soft d/g/r/t/m/n; no hard clusters; liquids (l, r) flow freely
- Vowels: open Italian-style vowels; final -a/-i/-o preferred; no umlaut
- Suffixes: -ma, -ri, -in, -lin, -olin (Latin-derived nominal endings)
- Compounding: single polysyllabic stem; no hyphenation or apostrophe
- Use for: trade-city humans, coastal peoples, mercantile cultures

**Worked example:** *Vernali* — Ver- (labial root) + -nali (Romance terminal); a personal name

---

## Family 2 — Slavic-adjacent

**Pool samples:** Tikhaya, Shontobi, Shyona, Vyanoweir, Bledreon, Husakas

- Consonants: sh/kh/bl/dr clusters; voiced sibilants; hard final consonants softened with -a/-ya
- Vowels: ya/ya/ei diphthongs; -aya / -obi nominal endings
- Suffixes: -aya (adjective-noun: Tikhaya), -obi (tribal: Shontobi), -weir (compound terminal: Vyanoweir), -reon (Bledreon)
- Compounding: root + adjective ending fused; no separate words
- Use for: steppe nomads, northern cold-climate peoples, clan-structured cultures

**Worked example:** *Blekhaya* — Ble- (Bledreon opener) + -kh- (Tikhaya consonant cluster) + -aya (Tikhaya terminal); a personal name

---

## Family 3 — Invented-harsh (guttural, foreign-feeling)

**Pool samples:** Oznak, Qord'ik, Qindo, Tarnak, Nuun, Neth, Wendi

- Consonants: Qk/zn/rd clusters; glottal apostrophe (Qord'ik = attested); final -k/-nak terminal
- Vowels: compressed short vowels (o, u, e); Nuun/Neth show doubled or minimal vowel
- Suffixes: -nak (place/people: Oznak, Tarnak), -ik (personal: Qord'ik), -nd (terminal stop)
- Apostrophe: marks a glottal stop mid-name; use sparingly — one per name maximum
- Use for: desert peoples, isolationist cultures, peoples with alien-feeling languages

**Worked example:** *Kruvnak* — Kruv- (invented harsh cluster) + -nak (attested terminal); a clan or settlement name

---

## Family 4 — English-descriptive (Common-tongue names)

**Pool samples:** Stone Men, The Guiltless, Shapers, Kuru (short/blunt edge)

- Structure: literal English noun/adjective phrases used as proper names; no constructed roots
- Pattern: often plural (Stone Men, The Guiltless) or agent-nouns (Shapers)
- Use for: cultures that name themselves by what they do/are; outsider-coined demonyms; ancient clans whose name is a folk-memory label
- Do NOT mix with Romance or harsh-invented roots — an English-descriptive name is a complete label, not a stem to suffix

**Worked example:** *The Steadfast* — a faction or people name; pure English-descriptive form
