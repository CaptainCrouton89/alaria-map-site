# Giant Onomastics

**Author anchor:** Two distinct self-naming registers — **Gül-Germanic** (Güli mountain giants) and **Greikh harsh-cluster** (Greikh city-giants) — plus English-descriptive outsider labels for unnamed subtypes. Do not blend the two registers. English-descriptive naming applies only to outsider-facing group labels, not to giant self-naming.

**Source pool (frozen map names — do not copy verbatim):**
- *Güli culture:* Güli, Gül Mountains, Nygül, Egül, Sorgül, Breidlheiss Basin, Gülheim — personal: Filgerran II, Morveth
- *Greikh culture:* Greikh, Malo Kon Greikh, Kregkard, Vrommol, Morgrad, Bragkur, Vog Dridril, Khurgril, Ulbrizil, Kebuz Bakh, Zeror Gokh, Xhodon, Egzahd, Zord Oshbag — geographic: Xcraya River, Xeltri Plateau
- *Sacred tongue (sparse):* Zetl Heski Voth, Zetl Hesk Rou
- *Outsider-descriptive:* Gorgers, Hill Giants, Flesh Giant, Gallows Giant, Ruin Giant — place: Giant Lands, Giant's Corridor, Giantfell, Giants Cradle, Prisoner Hills

## Güli Register (mountain giants)

### Sound palette
- Hallmark: **ü umlaut** as the ethnic/geographic root vowel; any name tied to the Güli culture or their mountains carries it
- Consonants: hard stops g/k/r, nasal-stop clusters (-rr-, -lv-), fricative gr/lgh
- Vowel behavior: compound words use diphthong ei (Breid-) in the Norse-borrowing layer; ü restricted to the Gül root and direct derivatives; -heim terminal is open
- Forbidden / off-style: Greikh harsh-cluster initials (Br/Kr/Vr/Xcr); umlaut on non-Gül morphemes; smooth Romance vowels (Salomor-class)

### Morphemes & affixes
- **Gül-** — the core root; names the people (Güli), the mountain range (Gül Mountains), and the capital (Gülheim); means "mountain" or "highland" in the Güli tongue
- Prefixes that attach to **-gül** to name sub-ranges: **Ny-** (Nygül, north), **E-** (Egül, east), **Sor-** (Sorgül, southeast — "Sor" = wild/dangerous)
- Germanic suffix: **-heim** (seat/stronghold of: Gülheim = stronghold of the Gül)
- Norse compound layer: **Breid-** (broad/wide, cf. Norse *breiðr*) + **-heiss** (pass/hot, cf. Norse *heitr*); Breidlheiss = "Broad Pasture" in the Güli tongue
- Personal name structure: harsh medial cluster + soft personal terminal — **-an** (masculine: Filgerran) / **-eth** (feminine: Morveth)

### Naming customs
- **Geographic names** compound a descriptor prefix directly onto -gül or add -heim: the morphology makes provenance legible to any Güli
- **Personal names** carry a harsh consonant cluster in the middle (Filg-err-an, Morv-eth) — softened only at the terminal; this mirrors how the Güli culture presents: hard-core, controlled at the edge
- **Clan names** (thin pool) likely follow the geographic model — a clan of the Egül range would take an Egül-derived token; extrapolate conservatively until more are attested
- Honorific "II" appended as a dynastic numeral in the human-facing record; not a Güli-tongue element

### Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Sogülheim** — fortress of the southern Gül: So- (south, minimal-change variant on Sor-) + -gül + -heim; a secondary Güli stronghold
- **Greldvan** — personal (masculine): Greld- (harsh cluster, -ld terminal mirroring Filgerran's inner -gerr-) + -an; a Güli warrior name
- **Halveth** — personal (feminine): Halv- (aspirated-lv cluster, parallel to Morv-) + -eth; a Güli elder name *(provisional — feminine personal pool is single sample)*

## Greikh Register (city-giants)

### Sound palette
- Hallmark: **multi-consonant initial cluster** — Br-, Kr-, Vr-, Xcr-/Xh-, Gr-, Kh-, Zr-, Dr- (within compound)
- Consonants: stops b/k/g/d + liquid r/l in cluster; fricatives zh/gh in medial/terminal position
- Vowel behavior: short central vowels (a, o, u) inside; terminals are typically hard stops or fricatives (-kard, -kur, -gril, -bakh, -gokh, -zahd, -zil)
- Forbidden / off-style: Güli ü umlaut; Norse ei diphthongs; -heim terminal; apostrophes or elven sibilant openings
- **Note on "Malo Kon":** the confederation title uses a softer M- initial and may be borrowed from a prestige tongue or a political register rather than the city-building vernacular; treat it as a proper-noun phrase, not a productive morpheme

### Morphemes & affixes
- **Cluster initials** are the primary identity marker: a new Greikh city name must open with a two-consonant cluster
- Terminals: -kard (cf. Kregkard — market/trade hall), -kur (cf. Bragkur — likely "hold/ground"), -gril (cf. Khurgril — outpost), -zil (cf. Ulbrizil — far/edge), -bakh (cf. Kebuz Bakh — compound: settlement + epithet), -gokh (cf. Zeror Gokh)
- Two-word city names (Zord Oshbag, Kebuz Bakh, Zeror Gokh, Vog Dridril) appear for larger or more significant settlements: first word = a place-stem, second word = a defining descriptor

### Naming customs
- City-states are self-named by their founding clans; outsiders use the same names (the Greikh don't use outsider labels for their own places)
- Single-word names (Kregkard, Vrommol, Khurgril) for ordinary city-states; two-word names for cities with a defining trait or a storied founding (Zord Oshbag, Vog Dridril)
- Personal names are thin in the corpus; by analogy with city structure, expect cluster-initial + hard terminal (masculine pattern) — but flag new personal names as **provisional** until more are attested

### Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Dravkur** — city-state: Dr- cluster + -av- + -kur (-kur terminal from Bragkur); a mountain-edge hold *(provisional)*
- **Grezil** — city-state: Gr- (from Greikh root) + -ez- + -zil (-zil terminal from Ulbrizil); a western outpost *(provisional)*

## Sacred / Ritual Register (sparse)

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Source pool:** Zetl Heski Voth ("Lake of First Breath"), Zetl Hesk Rou ("Lake of Last Breath")

The sacred tongue is entirely distinct from both Güli and Greikh vernaculars. **Zetl** appears to be a fixed term for sacred water (both examples are lakes). **Hesk(i)** is the adjectival qualifier; **Voth/Rou** likely encode birth/death polarity. The cluster Zetl- (Z + etl) is unique to this register. Do not derive new sacred names from this pool — with only two attested phrases, extrapolation produces speculation. Flag any sacred-register coins as provisional.

## Outsider-Descriptive Naming

**Not a giant register** — these are labels applied by smaller peoples, not self-naming:

- Group/subrace labels: Gorgers, Hill Giants (English noun phrases)
- Creature variants: Flesh Giant, Gallows Giant, Ruin Giant, Shambling Giants (English descriptive compounds)
- Place labels: Giant Lands, Giant's Corridor, Giantfell, Giants Cradle, Prisoner Hills

When authoring a new giant group that lacks a named culture, an English-descriptive outsider label is appropriate. When authoring a Güli or Greikh entity, use the correct cultural register instead.
