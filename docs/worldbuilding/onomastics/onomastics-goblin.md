# Goblin Onomastics

**Author anchor:** Hard consonant clusters, reduplication, German-tinted English-descriptive  
**Source pool (frozen map names — do not copy verbatim):** Stravlar, Xibli, Mudzwiti, Blitzling, Coghead, Aciabro, Darkling, Etherweaver, Rudding, Scalawag, Spine Goblin, Wydling — place names: Lyoki Ingi Ingi, Kobuk, Lethos, Melrox Island, Copper Hills, Dunes of Kunagi

## Sound palette
- Consonants that recur: hard clusters (str/xb/mz/dz), voiced stops (b/g/d), fricatives (v/z), terminal -r/-k/-x
- Vowel behavior: short compressed vowels (i, u, o) between consonant clusters; reduplication repeats the entire vowel+consonant unit (Lyoki Ingi Ingi = 3-unit reduplication)
- Forbidden / off-style: long elven diphthongs (ei, weir), Norse umlauts, any name that reads "noble" or "arcane" — goblins read raw and percussive

## Morphemes & affixes
- Prefixes: Str-/Xib-/Mudz-/Kob- (hard-cluster openers); no semantic meaning — phoneme-cluster identifiers
- Suffixes: -ling (German-English diminutive: Blitzling, Darkling, Wydling), -ding (Rudding — hard-stop variant), -lar/-witi/-ko (raw consonant or short-vowel terminals)
- Reduplication: full-word or 2-syllable unit repeated 2–3 times (Lyoki Ingi Ingi); signals a war cry, tribal name, or sacred location
- English-descriptive: Coghead, Etherweaver, Scalawag, Spine Goblin — plain English compound used as a name; typically outsider labels or specialist-clan identifiers

## Naming customs
- **Tribal / clan names** favor hard-cluster roots or reduplication; reduplication signals old/sacred lineage
- **Settlement names** split: goblin-native settlements use short hard-consonant names (Kobuk, Lethos); outsider-named areas use English-descriptive (Copper Hills, Dunes of Kunagi)
- **Personal names** tend toward hard 2-syllable forms (Xibli, Aciabro) or specialist-label style (Coghead = a tinkerer-goblin's name)
- **-ling suffix** in goblin names carries German weight (Blitzling = lightning-ling), not the soft halfling diminutive; the phoneme environment (hard consonant before) keeps them distinct
- Reduplication used as a name should remain unmodified — do not alter the repeated unit

## Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Stravzak** — hard cluster: Strav- (str+av cluster, cf. Stravlar) + -zak (zak terminal); a personal name
- **Struvling** — German-tinted: Struv- (str+uv cluster, cf. Stravlar) + -ling; a specialist-clan name
- **Vopi Vopi** — reduplication: Vop- root repeated as two-word form; a sacred site or war-cry name
