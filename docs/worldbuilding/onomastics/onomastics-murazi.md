# Murazi Onomastics

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Author anchor:** Liquid opener, open vowel chain, z-interior — smooth and unhurried, "flowing water" register  
**Source pool (frozen — do not copy verbatim):** Murazi (race name); Luquihn (desert name — possibly murazi-coined; treated as uncertain secondary reference)

## Sound palette
- **Murazi**: Mu + ra + zi — M opener, liquid r interior, open vowel chain (u-a-i), z immediately before terminal vowel
- **Luquihn** (uncertain provenance): L + u + quihn — L opener (liquid), u vowel, -quihn complex close; structurally parallel to Murazi if murazi-coined
- Consonants: liquid openers (m, l); z as interior medial consonant; restrained hard consonants elsewhere
- Vowel behavior: open vowels in sequence (u-a, u-i); no compressed short-vowel pairs; names feel unhurried — appropriate for an aquatic culture in an unhurried desert environment
- Forbidden / off-style: hard consonant stacks; z as opener; percussive or goblin-style clusters; anything dry or staccato that conflicts with the flowing-water register

## Morphemes & affixes
- Liquid opener: **M-** (Murazi); **L-** possibly (Luquihn if murazi-named) — both are liquid consonants
- Open vowel chain: u + a/i sequence (Murazi = mu-ra-zi; all three vowels open)
- **Z-medial**: appears inside multi-syllable names, never opening; -zi terminal in Murazi
- **-i terminal**: Murazi ends in i; the -ihn close of Luquihn may be cognate (ihn = i + consonant close)
- 3-syllable names seem typical (Murazi = 3 syllables)

## Naming customs
- Liquid openers (m, l) are consistent across both samples and suit the underground river culture — names flow like water
- Z-sound marks the interior, not the opening — the approach is indirect (the culture lives in hidden waterways)
- Names are 3 syllables; shorter forms may be informal or childhood names; longer forms mark elder water-keepers
- Pearl-trade culture may produce compound place names (river + oasis = two-word names), but personal names are single-word

## Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Mulazi** — personal: Mul- (cf. Mu- in Murazi with l-bridge inserted) + -azi (-azi terminal, transposing Murazi's ra+zi elements); a pearl-diver's name
- **Mazuri** — river/passage name: Ma- (Mu- with vowel shift) + -zuri (z + uri, transposing Murazi's interior elements); a named underground waterway
- **Lumazin** — formal/elder: Lu- (cf. Luquihn's opener) + -mazin (-ma+zin, Murazi-patterned interior with harder nasal terminal); a water-temple elder's name
