# Centaur Onomastics

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Author anchor:** Sibilant opener, consonant-heavy interior, diphthong terminal — proud and complex  
**Source pool (frozen — do not copy verbatim):** Siquestrya (polity), Centaur (race name)

## Sound palette
- **Siquestrya**: S + iq + uestr + ya — sibilant S opener, interior consonant cluster (qu + estr), -ya diphthong terminal
- **Centaur**: C/S + en + taur — sibilant opener (c as s), -en- interior, -aur diphthong terminal
- Both names open with an S/C sibilant and close with a diphthong (-ya, -aur, -ia variants)
- Interior consonant clusters are the register signature: -uestr-, -iqu-, -nt-; names are not simple
- Vowels: concentrated in diphthong terminals (-ya, -aur); interior vowels are short bridges inside clusters
- Forbidden / off-style: monosyllables; soft liquid-only names; names without an interior consonant cluster; open vowel sequences without the sibilant anchor

## Morphemes & affixes
- S-/C- opener: both confirmed samples open with this sibilant — treat it as near-mandatory
- Interior cluster: -uestr-/-iqu-/-nt-/-str- — the complexity of Siquestrya's interior is the pride register; do not simplify
- Diphthong terminal: **-ya** (Siquestrya), **-aur** (Centaur), variant **-ia** — the terminal diphthong is the consistent close
- 3–4 syllables typical (Siquestrya = 4, Centaur = 2 — personal names likely fall in between at 3)

## Naming customs
- Long, complex names signal rank — a centaur who abbreviates their name signals surrender of status
- The sibilant opener (S/C) is the identity root; no other race in this corpus routinely opens with S + interior cluster + diphthong terminal
- Polity and territory names follow the Siquestrya skeleton (full interior cluster + -ya/-ia terminal)
- Personal names likely use the Centaur skeleton (shorter: sibilant + short interior + diphthong terminal, ~2–3 syllables)
- Diphthong terminals are non-negotiable; a name ending in a hard stop reads as belonging to a different race

## Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Sevriya** — territory/grazing ground: Sev- (S + ev, softer interior than Siquestrya) + -riya (-r + iya, diphthong terminal variant of -ya); a seasonal herd range name
- **Cauthorn** — personal: Cau- (C + au diphthong echo of Centaur's -taur) + -thorn (-th+orn terminal — heavier close for a warrior); a fighter's name
- **Siquevar** — formal/ancestral: Sique- (full opener from Siquestrya) + -var (-var, contracted terminal variant replacing -strya); an elder herd-leader's title name
