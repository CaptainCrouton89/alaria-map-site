# Pixie Onomastics

> **sparse sample — extrapolate conservatively**

**Author anchor:** Short, soft, vowel-rich — z/l/i openers, -ko/-a/-el terminals; elf-adjacent register but distinctly truncated  
**Source pool (frozen — do not copy verbatim):** Ianovko, Ikriel, Luma, Zelle, Zelidia, Magera

## Sound palette
- Consonants that recur: soft fricatives (z, v), liquids (l, m, n), soft stops (k, g) — never hard-stacked; no consonant clusters
- Vowel behavior: open vowels (a, e, i) dominate; vowel sequences like -ia, -ova possible; names feel airy and brief
- Syllable count: 1–3 syllables is the norm; 4 syllables (Ianovko) marks formal or heritage names only
- Forbidden / off-style: consonant stacks, guttural openers (b/g at front), anything multi-syllable and resonant — that reads elf, not pixie; also avoid pure elf diphthong runs (Eloweir-style)

## Morphemes & affixes
- I-/Iy- opener: Ianovko, Ikriel — used across long and medium forms; the most versatile opener
- Z-/Ze- opener: Zelle, Zelidia — carries a darker or more cultish flavor (Zelle are theocrats); soft z only, never a cluster
- L-/Lu-: Luma — minimal liquid opener, the lightest form; single-syllable or dual-syllable personal names
- M- opener: Magera (ruler title) — suggests authority; three syllables with open -a terminal
- Terminals: **-ko** (Ianovko — the strongest pixie-identity terminal; absent from elf sheets), **-el** (Ikriel — elf-adjacent but note: Ikriel opens with hard Ik-, which no elf name does), **-a** (Luma, Magera, Zelidia — the default soft close), **-e/-le** (Zelle — compressed close)
- Internal -v-: Ianovko's -nov- interior; v appears inside longer forms, not as an opener

## Naming customs
- Short names are the default register — 1–3 syllables; 4 syllables is formal or ancestral
- The **-ko terminal** is the sharpest pixie identity signal: it appears in the subrace name pool and nowhere in elf sheets; use it to anchor pixie-unambiguous names
- **Z-opener** correlates with the more cultish / isolated sub-lineages (Zelle, Zelidia); avoid it for grove-keeper or frost-wanderer characters
- **-el terminal** (Ikriel) is permissible but always pair it with a non-elven opener (Ik-, hard stop) to prevent the name reading as elf
- Names do NOT use two-word compounds (that is elven); a pixie name is one word, short
- The fae origin explains the soft open vowels without making pixie names derivative — the key distinction from elves is **brevity and the -ko terminal**

## Worked examples (derived, not copied)
- **Veliko** — personal: Vel- (soft v+el, cf. Ikriel's -el but as an opener) + -iko (-i+ko diminutive compound terminal); a personal name with both elf-echo and pixie marker
- **Zimara** — formal/ancestral: Zi- (Z-opener, vowel shift from Zelle's Ze-) + -mara (-ma+ra, cf. Magera's structure without copying it); a high-lineage or priestess name
- **Lunik** — personal: Lu- (cf. Luma's opener) + -nik (-n+ik, soft k-close without the full -ko weight); a grove-tender's everyday name
