# Topic G — Coupled-plane / two-timeline worldbuilding

The Alaria plan claims that material history and shadow/Celestia history are causally coupled — the three-soul death continuously feeds three flip-side planes with three different durabilities, and `high-level.md` asserts the architecture is "genuinely original." This document tests that claim against published precedent and extracts the operational discipline the coupling needs to survive 10,000 entities.

Verdict, surfaced up front: the full Alaria synthesis is not attested. Components are. The single closest partial precedent is the Egyptian *ren* — the soul-name that ends when the name is forgotten. The closest mechanical precedent for material acts producing durable flip-side state is Glorantha's God Time / Mundane Time coupling.

## Precedents

### Glorantha — God Time and the Web of Arachne Solara

Greg Stafford's setting is the closest published mechanical precedent for "material acts create durable flip-side state that then back-reacts on material history." Arachne Solara's Cosmic Compromise sealed the gods outside Time in eternal mythic repetition; mortals remained inside Time with free will. HeroQuesting is the controlled crossing: a mortal physically enters the God Plane and re-enacts (or alters) a frozen mythic act, and material consequences follow. The God Plane is a "vast net woven out of the strands of myths — each the encapsulated deed of gods or heroes in the God Time" ([Web of Arachne Solara, Well of Daliath](https://wellofdaliath.chaosium.com/the-web-of-arachne-solara/)). Jeff Richard: "When we interact with the Godtime, we mortals can change our experience of it, thereby changing the myth. That becomes the new story and the new baseline" ([Heroquest: What is It?, Well of Daliath](https://wellofdaliath.chaosium.com/heroquest-what-is-it/)).

What this is not: a continuous mass-exchange. The God Plane is a deep structural archive, not a living civilization with its own weather and politics. Worship (continuous) follows existing strands; only deliberate heroquest (threshold) inscribes new ones.

### Sandman — the Dreaming

Gaiman's coupling is continuous and bidirectional. Every sleeper feeds the Dreaming; the Dreaming's accumulated content (Lucien's library of unwritten books, the geography, the residents) is constituted by that feed. The flip side has its own politics — Morpheus's decision to kill Orpheus, a material-plane act, triggers the Furies' attack on the Dreaming, leading to Dream's death and replacement by Daniel and a "reality storm that rippled across time and space" (*The Kindly Ones*, 1996). Each Endless is "merely patterns... ideas... wave functions... repeating motifs" (Destruction, *Brief Lives*, 1994). The Endless are domains; the lord is the law.

What holds the coupling: ontological asymmetry. The Dreaming exists only because minds sleep. It cannot absorb the waking world because it is structurally downstream of the waking world. A mortal who gains architectural authority over it — a Dream Vortex — is the failure case, the moment when the planes begin to collapse into one.

### Le Guin — the Dry Land

The Dry Land in *The Farthest Shore* (1972) and *The Other Wind* (2001) is the cleanest "death-plane coupling that breaks and must be repaired" precedent in fantasy. Ancient mages broke the original compact between humans and dragons by "cast[ing] spells that stole some of the beautiful Western Lands from the dragons, for men to go to after death." Each stone in the boundary wall is a magical-theft act. In *The Other Wind*, the dead are trapped — they cannot move on, they pull at the living through dreams, and the wall must be physically unmade stone by stone.

Le Guin's "Earthsea Revisioned" lecture (Oxford, 1992) names the underlying problem: the original Dry Land was a borrowed image — Greek Hades, Rilke, Dante — that did not fit Earthsea's actual Taoist value system. The coupling failed because the two planes were designed on incompatible metaphysical models. The fix in *The Other Wind* is to restore the death plane to the same cycle of transformation that governs the living world.

### Pratchett — Death's realm

Death's domain has its own ongoing operations: the timers, the library, Mort's apprenticeship, the cottage at *Mon Repos*. *Reaper Man* (1991) is the diagnostic: when Death is removed, the unmoved life force "starts to build up," producing poltergeists, mall-creatures, and other material symptoms. The timers are particularly interesting — each material birth creates a new physical object in Death's realm, and the object's emptying constrains material lifespan. This is a thin but real mass-exchange. *Hogfather* (1996) puts the worship-economy alongside it: the Auditors attack the Hogfather because they want to destroy belief-sustained entities.

### Susanna Clarke — *Piranesi* (2020)

The House is "formed by ideas, knowledge, and powers" that flowed out of the primary world and were forgotten. Statues populate the House; each represents something the outside world lost. Access requires a specific rite. Long-term inhabitants forget themselves — Matthew Rose Sorenson becomes Piranesi. Ketterley's parasitic use of the coupling (importing rivals to harvest their expertise while they forget it) is the failure case. The coupling holds because the House does not merge with the outside world: it *consumes* those who enter too fully.

The House is close to mass-exchange on paper — material-world losses accumulate as House-state — but the accumulation is largely pre-existing rather than continuously generated by ongoing material events.

### Gene Wolfe — Briah and Yesod

In *The Urth of the New Sun* (1987), Yesod judges Briah through bounded juridical exchange. Severian's union with the Hierogrammate Apheta produces "something tiny yet immense" — the White Fountain — which materially renews Briah's sun. The coupling is conditional, threshold-bound, and routed through a single named exchange agent each cycle. Hierodules are long-term Briah agents preparing for the trial. Wolfe is doing state-mirroring with periodic narrow exchange, not continuous mass-transit.

### Tolkien — Aman before and after the Bending of the World

Before the Downfall of Númenor, Aman was geographically continuous with Middle-earth, reachable by westward sailing. Souls of the Elven dead transited continuously to the Halls of Mandos; the Valar's responses to Middle-earth events (raising the Pelóri, deciding when to intervene) were continuous flip-side political life. The Bending converted physical coupling into metaphysical: only Elves, in hallowed ships, can navigate the Straight Road, which "left the physical world" (paraphrased in Tolkien Gateway, drawn from *The Silmarillion* "Akallabêth" and *Morgoth's Ring*). Tolkien himself in the "Myths Transformed" texts (*Morgoth's Ring*, 1993) acknowledged the physical coupling was a problem — Valinor as a sailable continent required cataclysmic narrative to enforce its inaccessibility. The Straight Road moves the separation from geography to ontology.

### N.K. Jemisin — the Broken Earth trilogy

Father Earth is not a separate plane — he is the material world's geology, made conscious. His grievance over the Moon's loss (the original mass-exchange event that went catastrophically wrong) drives material Fifth Seasons. Stone Eaters are the autonomous mediators. The architecture is consciousness-within-the-physical, not parallel-plane, but Jemisin's diagnostic is the same as Le Guin's: a coupling built on extraction destabilizes when the extracted party cannot sustain the burden.

### Holdstock — *Mythago Wood* (1984)

Ryhope Wood generates mythagos from the accumulated archetypal material in nearby human unconscious minds. Multiple incarnations of the same archetype (Guiwenneth, Arthur) accumulate simultaneously. Mythagos exit the wood, interact with people physically, and metabolize visitors into mythic agents themselves. The coupling is continuous but proximity-dependent — "mythagos slowly deteriorate and die when distant from the wood." Holdstock's flip side has its own wars and loves, generated from material storytelling.

### Egyptian ka / ba / akh / ren / shut

Five to nine soul-parts, not three, but with genuinely differentiated fates. The ka stays in the tomb, fed by offerings. The ba travels nightly between the corpse and the Duat. The akh is the immortal end-state formed once the heart passes judgment. The shut is the shadow. The *ren* — the name — exists "as long as the name of the being exists" ([World History Encyclopedia, "The Soul in Ancient Egypt"](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1023/the-soul-in-ancient-egypt/)). Deliberate name-erasure (damnatio memoriae) was a permanent metaphysical destruction. Assmann (*Death and Salvation in Ancient Egypt*, Cornell, 2005) titles a section "One Lives, if His Name is Mentioned." The ren is the single closest precedent in any tradition for a soul-component ending when remembrance fails.

But the ren is not the identity-carrier in Egyptian theology. Personality lives in the ba and the heart (ib). The ren is more a social-cosmic tag than the *you* that makes you you. Alaria's distinctive move is making the personality-bearer the fragile, forgetting-terminated component — inverting the Egyptian configuration.

### Exalted (White Wolf, 2001) — hun and po

The most-cited RPG precedent for a multi-soul system. Mortals have two parts: hun (higher soul, intellect/memory) goes to Lethe for reincarnation with memory wiped; po (lower soul) dissolves into the earth. A hun that refuses Lethe becomes an Underworld ghost. Celestial Exalts carry an Exaltation Shard as a third memory-bearing component, but this is not universal for mortals. The worship-economy (gods sustained by prayer) is on a different plane (Yu-Shan) from the death-economy (Underworld). The systems do not converge through one boundary.

### Pullman — *His Dark Materials*

Body, dæmon, ghost — three parts, three fates. The dæmon dissolves at death "like atoms of smoke." The ghost persists in the prison-world of the dead until Lyra and Will cut the opening at the climax of *The Amber Spyglass* (2000), and the ghosts dissolve into the atoms of the universe. The ghost is personality-bearing — ghosts converse with Lyra, recognize each other, retain memory of life. A reading that puts identity in the dæmon (because the dæmon is the lived self during life) is textually available; the climactic equation between dæmon-dispersal and self-dispersal supports it. Either way, Pullman's mechanism does not terminate the identity-bearer when remembrance fails — the prison-world is populated by uncounted forgotten dead, and their eventual release is a chosen completion rather than a forgetting-termination. Pullman therefore matches the three-part-split formally but not the load-bearing forgetting-extinction mechanic.

### Other traditions surveyed without precedent for the full coupling

Plato's tripartite soul unifies into one immortal soul that reincarnates; the parts do not travel separately. Norse hugr/hamingja/fylgja have differential fates (the fylgja ends, hugr reincarnates into family, hamingja inherits) but no identity-ends-when-forgotten mechanic. Chinese hun/po (the source of Exalted's system) is two-way. Gnostic Pleroma is one-way emanation with selective return. Zelazny's Amber Shadows are one-directional projection. Malazan Warrens are extracted from rather than deposited into. Lewis's *Great Divorce* establishes transit without accumulation.

Four further cases that look closer on first glance — surveyed in the gap pass — do not hold up. The Yoruba ori/emi/ese tripartition has neglected ancestors lose influence, but the ori (the identity-soul) cycles into a new life; it is not annihilated by forgetting ([Idowu, *Olodumare: God in Yoruba Belief*, 1962](https://www.oriire.com/article/journeying-beyond-unraveling-the-yoruba-belief-of-the-afterlife); Bascom, in secondary literature). *The Locked Tomb*'s lyctoral soul-consumption runs opposite to Alaria's mechanism — in *Harrow the Ninth* (2020) Harrow deliberately suppresses Gideon's memory, and Gideon's consciousness narrates from within anyway. Hindu *sūkṣma-śarīra* has a three-body schema (sthūla, sūkṣma, kāraṇa) with differential durabilities, but the ahaṃkāra travels with the subtle body through reincarnation and dissolves at moksha through self-realization, not through external forgetting ([Flood, *An Introduction to Hinduism*, 1996](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahamkara); Sāṃkhya-Kārikā). Tolkien's *Athrabeth Finrod ah Andreth* (in *Morgoth's Ring*, 1993) is the deepest Tolkien text on differential soul-fate — elven fëa bound to Arda, human fëa exits Arda at death — but Andreth's "dying we die, and we go out to no return" describes departure from the world, not fading through neglect.

## Techniques that work

### Ontological asymmetry — the planes exist in different modes

Gloranthan gods are frozen-eternal; mortals are temporal-free; merging the two states is meaningless because each mode requires the other's absence. The Dreaming is constituted by sleepers — it cannot absorb the waking world because it depends on the waking world's continued sleeping. Tolkien's Elves use the Straight Road because their immortal nature is the gate; mortals cannot follow because mortality is the gate's filter. Each plane must have a *kind* of existence the other cannot sustain. For Alaria specifically: Celestia hosts both the daemons (who are sustained by living worship) and the spirits (who persist only as long as named). Both Celestia residents are constituted by the material plane's ongoing remembrance. If Celestia ever acquires a category of native, self-sustaining inhabitant, the asymmetry breaks and the coupling slackens. Hold the line — Celestia cannot generate its own life.

### Named threshold exchange — specify the aperture, not the connection

Piranesi's House admits visitors only through Arne-Sayles's rite. The Straight Road requires hallowed ships. The Dry Land's boundary is a wall of named stones, each a specific magical act. Glorantha's God Plane is reached through ritually established sacred ground. World of Darkness quantifies it as the Gauntlet, a numerical barrier-strength per location. For Alaria, the test: which events constitute the exchange points? Mortal death is the canonical aperture — three-strand sundering, each strand routed to a different plane. But major historical acts (Lyzaria's First Death, daemon ascensions, the Ezz Rift) are themselves aperture-events at planetary scale. The plan-language ("ask what it sent across") names the question but not the aperture. Settle it: every world-historical event in `era` or `event` weight `major` and above declares a flip-side consequence, or it does not pass canon-lint.

### The residue principle — one-sided accumulation that obligates both planes

Lucien's library of unwritten books is the cleanest example. The material world *loses* unwritten potential; the Dreaming *holds* it as a permanent archive. Le Guin's wall is built from stolen dragon territory — both planes need the wrong resolved. Jemisin's Moon is the residue of a broken exchange whose absence drives both Earth's grievance and humanity's instability. Glorantha's Web accumulates mythic deeds. For Alaria the residue is already specified — soul, shadow, spirit. The three-soul split is the residue mechanism. What needs reinforcement is the *back-reaction*: the material plane has to feel the pressure of accumulated Celestia spirits and Malstaris shadows in ways the canon makes legible. If the corpus never shows a material event being shaped by remembered-spirit pressure or unforgiven-shadow weight, the residue stops mattering and Failure Mode 2 begins.

### Sovereignty by scope, not by geography

Gaiman's Endless are sovereign within their domain and powerless outside it. Death cannot command Dream. Dream cannot command Death. Each domain's lord *is* the domain's law. Gloranthan gods rule God Time absolutely and Mundane Time not at all — the Compromise stripped them of mundane jurisdiction. Earthsea wizards can briefly cross into the Dry Land but exercise no power there. The Alaria question is whether Aurus and Nydus (suns) and the daemon lords are scope-sovereigns in this sense. Aurus is the source of souls and the Astral terminus — that is scope-sovereignty. Nydus is symmetric on the Malstaris side. The daemons, who live in Celestia and act *in the material plane* through projected Deoric, are the architectural risk: cross-planar agency is the mechanism that collapses boundaries elsewhere. The current cosmology survives this because daemon Deoric is costly and bounded, but the rule needs to be load-bearing in the canon-lint as the daemon roster grows.

### Name the heresy

The God Learners are the indispensable example. Stafford built Glorantha so the canon includes a class of figures who tried to make the sacred plane operational — they treated Runes as interchangeable variables, swapped goddesses between regions, triggered famines and the Closing of the Seas. The failure mode is *in the world*, where it gives weight to the coupling's rules. Le Guin's wall-mages serve the same function: in-world hubris that explains why the death plane is currently broken. Alaria already has a candidate equivalent — the lore around the Labyrinth points toward a Tarkhetan or Neferati heresy of attempted leyline-cartography. Author it. A named scholar (or polity, or sect) whose attempt to systematize the coupling failed catastrophically gives the rules retrospective weight that pure cosmological assertion never carries.

## Failure modes

### Failure mode 1 — the flip side gets all the cool stuff

The mirror plane accumulates named NPCs with persistent ideologies, while the material plane offers only generic encounter space. Planescape institutionalizes this — natives of the Prime are called "primes" or "clueless" ([babbling-brooks.com retrospective, 2024](https://babbling-brooks.com/2024/03/18/adds-planescape-the-good-the-bad-and-the-mixed-bags/); Maliszewski's *Grognardia* retrospective, November 2024). *The Great Modron March*: "planar adventuring ain't about slaughtering monsters or crawling through dungeons." The setting encodes contempt for the material plane in its own cant. The 4e Feywild/Shadowfell repeat the pattern by design — Mike Mearls describes the material plane as the "normal band" between two emotionally extreme realms ([D&D Beyond, "Shadowfell and Feywild Explained"](https://www.dndbeyond.com/posts/43-d-ds-shadowfell-and-feywild-explained)). Sandman runs the same risk — the literary analysis on comicbookmovie.com finds Barbie's dream self is "more valid and true than anything she feels while waking" — but offsets it with standalone waking-world stories that earn their own weight ("24 Hours," the Shakespeare arcs).

The design move that produces this: concentrate persistent, ideologically complex inhabitants on the flip side while leaving the material plane as a gazetteer. Encode the hierarchy in the setting's vocabulary.

### Failure mode 2 — nobody cares about the second timeline

The flip side is technically present but its events don't structurally obligate the material plane's response. D&D 5e Feywild/Shadowfell illustrate this most cleanly: documented at [Flutes Loot](https://www.flutesloot.com/echoes-of-the-material-world/) — "the Feywild and Shadowfell have been largely ignored in 5e source material, providing very little guidance to DMs on what these planes are." Mechanics exist; adventure structure does not require their use. Tel'aran'rhiod in *The Wheel of Time* edges toward the same failure: the coupling rules are specified (wounds carry, death is permanent), but information found in the dream world rarely binds the material plot ([Theoryland Research, "The Unseen World"](https://www.theoryland.com/studies.php?page=unseenworld)). The Malazan warrens proliferate until reader attention can no longer track which warren is which — Speculiction's 2011 review of *Dust of Dreams* notes that even a direct in-universe explanation "required the reader to think back upon all they had learned of the subject in previous volumes" ([Speculiction, 2011](http://speculiction.blogspot.com/2011/11/slow-exhale-consistency-of-malazan-book.html)).

The design moves that produce this: make flip-side events reversible from the material side; refuse to embed the coupling into adventure structure; proliferate planes until individual distinctness dilutes.

### Failure mode 3 — too coupled, no thematic contrast

Free travel and shared mechanical vocabulary collapse the distinction. The babbling-brooks Planescape critique notes that even in exotic planar towns, gold remains the currency — the rhetoric of cosmological difference rides on mechanical sameness. D&D's Shadowfell and Feywild are described as "echoes" of the material plane, "sometimes very accurately reflecting the other plane, and sometimes just a vague gesture in the general direction" — echo-geography produces uncanny effect on first encounter and flattens on repeat ([Flutes Loot](https://www.flutesloot.com/echoes-of-the-material-world/)). Glorantha's God Learners are the in-world canonical case: by treating God Time as a database of interchangeable Runes, they collapsed the sacred/mundane distinction and brought down famines and the Closing of the Seas ([Andrew Logan Montgomery, "The Sin of the God Learners," 2019](https://andrewloganmontgomery.blogspot.com/2019/09/the-sin-of-god-learners-metaphysics-of.html)). Tolkien's pre-Bending Aman ran into this in reverse — the planes were too literally coupled (a sailable continent), so cataclysm was required to enforce the separation. The "Myths Transformed" revision moved the separation from geography to ontology.

The design moves that produce this: allow free repeatable travel; reuse currency, magic, and motivation structures on both sides; systematize the sacred plane's rules until they become identical to material-plane rules; design flip-side geography as direct echo of material geography.

## Recommendations for Alaria

### Originality verdict — PARTIAL, leaning ORIGINAL

The full Alaria synthesis is not attested in any surveyed work. Three claims have to be split apart:

- *The three-way split with three distinct destinations and three distinct durabilities.* Egyptian and Norse traditions split souls more than two ways with differential fates. Pullman gives three parts with three fates. None do exactly three streams to three distinct cosmic planes in the Alaria configuration.
- *The identity-bearer is the shortest-durability stream, and ends when forgotten.* The Egyptian *ren* is the single closest precedent — names that end when erased or unmentioned. But the ren is not the personality-bearer in Egyptian theology; personality lives in the ba and the ib. Alaria's specific move — putting the *you* in the fragile, forgetting-terminated component — appears without close precedent. The gap pass on Yoruba ori, Hindu ahaṃkāra, Pullman's dæmon-or-ghost, and Tolkien's *Athrabeth* confirms this: each has differential soul-fate, none ends the identity-bearer through external forgetting. The genuinely original move in Alaria is this inversion.
- *Worship-economy + death-economy + historical-cause coupling, all running across the same plane boundary.* Glorantha integrates the three economies but distributes them across different boundaries (God Time, Underworld, Hero Plane). Discworld has the worship-economy strongly but not the death/identity coupling. Pullman has the three-part death without the worship economy. No surveyed precedent runs all three through one plane boundary the way Alaria does in Celestia (where daemons live, fed by living prayer, and spirits arrive after death to persist only as long as their names are remembered).

The orchestrator should note: the originality claim survives, but the specific original element is narrower than the high-level plan implies. The *coupling itself* (material acts feed flip-side state) has Glorantha and Sandman as substantial precedent. The *three-soul split* has Egyptian and Pullman as substantial precedent. What is not precedented is the combination plus the inversion of putting identity in the shortest-lived stream.

`high-level.md` says: "This coupling is genuinely original — lean on it." The accurate version is: lean on the Celestia configuration (worship + death + remembrance on one plane) and on the spirit-fades-when-forgotten move. The mass-exchange framing has direct precedent in Glorantha and Sandman, and should not be the marketed-original element.

### The coupling needs an edge kind, not just authorial discipline

The plan instructs authors to "ask what it sent across" when authoring material history. The precedents argue this is insufficient at corpus scale. Specifying the *aperture* is what published settings do that loose coupling does not. The existing `cosmology` family has `flipSideOf` (state-mirroring, entity-to-entity, e.g. a material region's Celestia counterpart) and `sourceOf` (one entity originates another). Neither covers "this event transferred substance to that plane." `caused` (history family) does causal sequence but is plane-agnostic.

Proposal: a new `cosmology/sentAcross` edge, authored on a material `event` or `era`, target being the flip-side entity (plane, region, or event) that received the transfer. `note` carries what was sent — souls, shadows, spirits, daemonic substance, leyline residue. This does not overlap `flipSideOf` (which is static state-mirror) or `sourceOf` (which is unbounded origination); it specifically records *transfer at an event*. Canon-lint then gains a check: any `event` of weight `major` or above with no `sentAcross` edge produces a warning until coverage stabilizes.

Open issue to settle before adoption: the entity-relations rule "author from the smaller/more-specific entity" assumes the source entity exists. Events are first-class entities in the corpus (`entityType: event`), so this works mechanically, but the convention has not yet been exercised for cosmology edges on event entities. Resolve this before locking the proposal into `entity-relations.md`. The submission report flags this as a proposed but not yet locked change.

### The Labyrinth — resolve the name collision, then author the empty room

The corpus carries two Labyrinths whose relationship is not stated. `artifact-the-labyrinth.md` is the Izzus-leyline structure beneath Evertorch cathedral — a folded-time corridor under a Tarkhon city. The flip-side Labyrinth in `high-level.md` and `progress.md` is a parallel region alongside Malstaris and Celestia, with no entity file. The author-notes on the Evertorch artifact explicitly defer the question of who made the structure and whether further Izzus fissures (Tornia merchants, the Kerwin king's longevity) are the same thread.

The first move is to decide whether these are the same Labyrinth or two unrelated entities sharing a name. The precedents favor coupling them: an Izzus-leyline structure that surfaces at Evertorch and also exists as a cosmic region on the flip side is exactly the residue principle in action — a Labyrinth that accumulates time-distortion on both sides of the boundary, with the cathedral basement being one of its material apertures. This reading also gives the flip-side region a concrete material handle, which prevents it from drifting into pure abstraction (Failure Mode 2).

Once coupled, the precedents converge on the same authoring rule: the Labyrinth should be the *un-archived* region. Glorantha's God Plane is intelligible because Arachne Solara wove its Web; the Labyrinth should be the region the Web does not cover. Sandman's Dreaming has architecture because Morpheus is its lord; the Labyrinth should be the region with no sovereign and no fixed geometry. The artifact file already enforces this — corridors of inconsistent length, surveyors who do not agree with themselves. Extend the same discipline to the flip-side region.

Practical authoring: a single `plane-labyrinth.md` entity that establishes the Labyrinth as the unmapped, unsovereigned part of the flip side, with a `cosmology/flipSideOf` edge tying it to `artifact-the-labyrinth` and a body that explicitly refuses to name inhabitants or fix geography. Every later attempt to fill it should declare what it consumes or what it refuses to hold. The matching heresy is already implied in the Evertorch lore — the Tarkhon clergy's attempted surveys. Author the scholar (see next section) and the Labyrinth's resistance to being archived becomes a recurring corpus motif rather than a single entity's deferred footnote.

### The failure mode Alaria is most exposed to

Failure Mode 1 — the flip side getting all the cool stuff. The cosmology is "strong" per `high-level.md`; the floor is 3,500 places, half of which are ≤15-word stubs. The same imbalance that makes Planescape's Prime Material feel like a setup is present in the starting state: a richly-authored flip side (Malstaris, Celestia, Astral, Eindumor; three-soul death; daemon economics with set numbers; Lyzaria as architect of mortality) sitting above a thin material gazetteer. The pincer method is meant to fix this from the floor up, but the cosmology will continue to be the cooler-feeling layer until the middle is built out.

The specific defensive move from the precedents: do not let the flip-side planes accumulate named, ideologically complex daemons faster than the material plane accumulates named, ideologically complex mortals. The 60–150 daemon target in `high-level.md` is the danger zone — if daemons get faces and biases before mortals do, the corpus will read as Sigil-with-an-attached-Prime. Lock the seven-major scholar roster (item 2) and the daemon-mortal authoring ratio before adding more daemons.

### Scholar attribution — Velorin owns the cosmology, with two proposals for approval

`scholar-voices.md` assigns Velorin of Istora cosmology — flip-side structure, three planes of death, daemon lifecycle. Velorin is the right primary owner. Two scholar-attribution moves are surfaced here as proposals, not action items. The `scholar-attribution.md` convention is explicit that attribution (especially staged disagreement) is approval-gated. Both proposals require the user's sign-off before any entity file uses them.

Proposal one: stage a Velorin / Temavori disagreement on the meaning of Lyzaria's First Death. The coupling is a historical claim as much as a cosmological one — Lyzaria carved the channels every later death flows through. Temavori (Golden Age cosmogony, Lyzaria-era material) reads it elegiacally; Velorin (cosmological grammar) reads it as architecture. The question "does the spirit end because the name fades, or does the name fade because the spirit ends?" is itself the substance of the coupling — the Egyptian *ren* tradition surfaces the same ambiguity. Staged disagreement here would do real work, not paper over a gap. Surface for approval before authoring.

Proposal two: add a niche scholar whose specialty is failed Labyrinth surveys — the in-world heresy in the God Learner mold. One new person entity, tagged `scholar`, weight `minor`, attached to the Tarkhetan or Neferati clergy implied in the Evertorch lore. Their attempted maps would be cited as the *reason* the Labyrinth's geography cannot be settled, the inverse of Oblexan's bills-of-sale empiricism. This scholar would be a single-purpose voice — Labyrinth content only — and would carry the "named heresy" technique into the corpus. Surface for approval before adding the person entity.

### Foundations-item action items

- *Item 1 (pantheon).* Hold the daemon roster authoring until item 2 (scholars) is locked and a `cosmology/sentAcross` edge kind is decided. Daemons live in Celestia and are coupling participants — they cannot be sized before the coupling's edge vocabulary is settled. Otherwise Failure Mode 1 begins immediately.
- *Item 2 (scholars).* Author the Velorin/Temavori coupling disagreement as one of the seven majors' load-bearing rivalries. Add the niche Labyrinth-cartographer scholar.
- *Item 4 (era entities).* Lyzaria's First Death and the Ezz Rift are the two coupling-foundation events. Their era entities must declare what they sent across — this is the test bed for the proposed `sentAcross` edge.
- *Item 5 (canon-lint).* Add a check: for any event of weight `major` or above, warn if no `sentAcross` edge exists. Severity warning, not error, until corpus coverage stabilizes.
- *Item 6 (pincer prototype).* Whichever region is selected, author its flip-side counterpart in lockstep. If the Tarkhon Empire is chosen, the Izzus-leyline Labyrinth (already in the corpus as an artifact) is the natural test case for material/flip-side coupling at the regional scale.
