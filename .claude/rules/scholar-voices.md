# Scholar Voices — Roster Reference

See `scholar-attribution.md` for blockquote syntax, linter rules, and when-to-attribute mechanics —
including the rule that coherence comes first and scholarly attribution is approval-gated, not a
default move.

This file is a roster reference for the cases where attribution has already been approved. It does not
authorize attribution; it ensures that when a voice IS used, it is the right one. Picking the wrong
scholar means the wrong bias and a falsely staged debate. The seven majors own distinct eras and
cross-cutting themes; niche experts own single wars, regions, or taxa. Never substitute a major for a
niche specialist when a specialist exists.

---

## The Seven Majors

### Temavori of Drachma — `person-temavori`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Golden Age of Man; cosmogony; full pantheon; God War; Long Winter mechanics |
| Culture | Romance-tongue human, southern coastal Drachma |
| Patron daemon | Dyos (slain Golden Age daemon) |
| Voice / bias | Consecrated grief. The Golden Age was the world's true dispensation; its end is an unrepaired catastrophe. The Age of Craggus is a flawed copy — structurally sound, missing everything that mattered. |
| Rhetorical tic | Backdates the Golden Age's theological sophistication by tens of thousands of years; frames every claim through loss. |
| Rivalries | Erindath: where Erindath sees the Age of Craggus as restoration, Temavori sees a failed substitute. Shoryaven dismisses him as "elegiac and therefore unreliable." |

### Erindath of Sennos — `person-erindath-of-sennos`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Age of Craggus; reborn-world consolidation; spread of Craggus worship; Laughing Plague |
| Culture | Latin-branch dwarf, civic-hall archives, Sennos |
| Patron daemon | Craggus |
| Voice / bias | Civic annalist. Treats the Age of Craggus as the natural order reasserting itself. Pro-dwarf; treats human accounts as secondary at best. Attributes more divine intention to Craggus than evidence supports. |
| Rhetorical tic | Labels everything outside direct verification as "rumor"; frames pro-Craggus conclusions as simply what the record shows. |
| Rivalries | Oblexan dismisses him explicitly ("never once asked for a bill of sale"). Temavori reads his era as a flawed copy of what was lost. Shoryaven calls him "a Craggus-cult ledger-keeper." Grømdrak of Gondurak is cited by him twice, both times with visible reluctance. |

### Oblexan — `person-oblexan`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Lost Ages (material dimension); False God's reign; Blight of Arcanus; Dark Night; Oblivion Years |
| Culture | Gnome, Pelican Isles |
| Patron daemon | Ghet (golem daemon; freedom and truth) |
| Voice / bias | Field empiricist. Trusts only what can be touched, measured, or dated. Dismisses mythology, oral tradition, and received authority as noise. Her greatest blind spot: events that left no ruins. |
| Rhetorical tic | Demands bills of sale. Calls Shoryaven's expansionism "the archaeology of irrelevance"; dismisses Velorin's cosmology as "poetry dressed as cosmology." |
| Rivalries | Erindath (explicit dismissal). Shoryaven (mutual contempt). Velorin (foundational — they disagree not on facts but on what counts as evidence). Nagasheni's account of the Gaeaic Eon is irreconcilable with Oblexan's material survey; neither resolved it. Ghrythan respects her field methods; the feeling is genuine and mutual. |

### Shoryaven — `person-shoryaven`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Great Expansion; Frost Fall; Return of Dragons (as political crisis); formation of modern polities |
| Culture | Slavic-adjacent human, northern steppe clans |
| Patron daemon | Bylzar (propagation, continuity, gold) |
| Voice / bias | Triumphalist. History is the world's continuous ascent toward the Great Expansion as its destination. Elder-race accounts are inferior to human-authored records; anything pre-Expansion is peripheral. |
| Rhetorical tic | Dismisses rivals by genre: Temavori = "elegiac"; Erindath = "ledger-keeper"; Oblexan = "archaeology of irrelevance." Borrows from Tybalexyn's sky-charts without attribution. |
| Rivalries | Temavori, Erindath, Oblexan (all dismissed). Pembling ("He counts the gold. He does not count the graves."). Ghrythan's life project is explicitly countering his "ascent of human civilization" framing. |

### Pembling of Grendenheim — `person-pembling`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Seventh Dawn; Dark Ages from within; current-era polity founding traumas; post-Dark-Ages settlement |
| Culture | Halfling Germanic-branch, Grendenheim |
| Patron daemon | Lorus (daemon of darkness) |
| Voice / bias | Melancholy witness. The Dark Ages were a season of spiritual proximity to the weight of what the world refuses to remember; the Seventh Dawn's recovery is a retreat. Constitutionally incapable of treating a current event without tracing its founding trauma. |
| Rhetorical tic | "He counts the gold. He does not count the graves." Finds Shoryaven not merely wrong but obscene. |
| Rivalries | Shoryaven (explicit moral condemnation). No recorded rivalry with the pre-Expansion majors — his era barely overlaps theirs. |

### Velorin of Istora — `person-velorin`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Cosmology (all eras): flip-side structure; three planes of death (Astral, Malstaris, shadow-layer); daemon lifecycle; cosmological implications of major historical events |
| Culture | Elf, Istora (ancient even by elven reckoning) |
| Patron daemon | Azak (time, clairvoyance, omens) |
| Voice / bias | Cosmological mythographer. The cosmos is a text continuously inscribed since before the Early Times; mythographic sources (oral traditions, dream-records, sacred narratives) are primary data, not secondary noise. |
| Rhetorical tic | Reads every historical event for its cosmological grammar. Oblexan dismissed her *Treatise* as "poetry dressed as cosmology." She spent four hundred years revising her major work. |
| Rivalries | Oblexan (foundational — they disagree on the nature of evidence itself, not on specific facts). Her framework predates Davorin's by eight thousand years; he extends it productively. |

### Ghrythan — `person-ghrythan`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era / theme | Races; migrations; languages; oral tradition (Lost Ages through modern settlement); cultures with no written record |
| Culture | Orc, tribal voiceholder tradition |
| Patron daemon | Solas (cunning, wit, humor) |
| Voice / bias | Polemical archivist. Treats the academy as an adversary to outmaneuver. Demonstrates through precision — cross-referencing oral genealogies against written record — that the written record systematically erased non-human cultural memory. |
| Rhetorical tic | Not outrage but precision: finds the exact gap in the written record where the oral tradition remembers something inconvenient. Blind spot: cultures that did not preserve oral memory either. |
| Rivalries | Shoryaven (explicit life's project to counter his framing). Genuine respect for Oblexan's field methods — surprising to both parties. |

## When to Cite Whom

| Claim is about… | Cite |
|----------------|------|
| Golden Age cosmogony, the full pre-Winter pantheon, God War mechanics, Long Winter | Temavori |
| Age of Craggus founding, reborn-world polities, Craggus-worship spread, Laughing Plague | Erindath |
| Lost Ages built environment, material culture, False God's administrative reach, Blight of Arcanus | Oblexan |
| Great Expansion events, Frost Fall disruption/recovery, Return of Dragons (political), modern polity formation | Shoryaven |
| Dark Ages lived texture, Seventh Dawn founding traumas, current-era polity grief | Pembling |
| Cosmological mechanics (any era): flip-side, three-soul death, daemon lifecycle, Astral/Malstaris | Velorin |
| Racial migrations, oral-tradition peoples, languages, non-literate cultural memory | Ghrythan |
| Cosmological event interpretation where Oblexan and Velorin would disagree | Stage both — this IS the disagreement |
| Shoryaven vs. Temavori/Erindath on pre-Expansion value | Stage the dismissal explicitly |

**For deep-niche claims** — a specific war's eyewitness, a regional genealogist, a creature
naturalist, a leyline specialist — use a niche expert from the index below, not a major. Majors are
cross-cutting; misapplying one flattens the specific expertise the niche scholars exist to provide.
