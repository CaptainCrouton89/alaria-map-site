# Originality at scale

There are thousands of settlements. The failure mode is sameness — every port a "bustling trade hub,"
every ruin "ancient and cursed." Guard against it:

- **Differentiate within a parent.** Before writing a town, skim its siblings (other children of the same parent). Don't repeat their hook, economy, or governing quirk. Each place needs one thing true *only* of it.
- **One concrete specific beats three adjectives.** A named export, a local law, a recurring problem — not "a proud people with a rich history."
- **No filler mystery.** "None know its origins" is banned unless a real answer exists underneath it.
- **Earn tropes or invert them.** A standard fantasy beat is fine once per region, not once per town. When an obvious trope tempts you, invert or constrain it.
- **Match length to importance.** Length tracks `zoomLevel`/`weight`: a low-zoom waypoint gets 2-3 tight sentences; reserve depth for places that carry story weight. Don't force an epic onto a hamlet.

Make places specific by pulling on the world's distinct levers — sky trade, leylines, the three-soul death, daemon worship — rather than generic fantasy furniture.
