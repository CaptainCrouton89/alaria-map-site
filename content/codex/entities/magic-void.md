Void is the element of absence, and it takes a moment to grant that absence is an element at all. The other eight are presences, flame and stone and water and light and their various partners. Void is the layer the rest define themselves against. A shaper channels emotion through Nilus, the void layer of the Elemental Planes, and what answers is nothing: a pocket of true absence laid into the world, where sound will not travel, fire finds nothing to feed on, and the air itself thins toward empty.

The trick of it is that the shaper destroys nothing. She borrows emptiness the way a fire-shaper borrows flame, and holds it over a space until the space contains nothing at all. Drop a void-pocket over a torch and the flame does not gutter so much as stop, starved of everything that let it be a flame. This makes void the natural answer to the other eight. An elementalist's working comes apart inside a void-pocket, because the medium it needed is simply gone. When two Kethic schools meet as enemies, the void-shaper is the one who ends the conversation.

Few people attune to it and fewer choose to. Void is hard to reach for, since the emotion a shaper aims has to be aimed at nothing in particular, and the ones who manage describe the work as cold and quieting in a way that does not wash off at the end of the day. The orders that teach it stay small and lean toward the austere. A void-shaper is usually hired for a single task: to stand near somebody else's magic and see to it that the magic does not work.
