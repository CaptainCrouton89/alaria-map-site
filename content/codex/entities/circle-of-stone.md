Like Stonehenge but far more imposing—colossal stones arranged in an ancient circle, each stone unbreakable and emanating massive magical power. The site sits at a convergence of Earth and Force leylines, allowing for extraordinarily powerful geomancy and force magic. The stones predate all known civilizations in the Northlands, and their creators remain a mystery.

The Circle is not a monument raised beside that convergence. It is the armature of an old working that seized it. Someone in the deep past channeled Golus and Dynus together to bind the crossing leylines into one sustained working, and in the binding bent them out of true. The stones are what that working left behind: matter that earth-shaping packed and force locked into a mass denser than stone can ordinarily be, which is why no tool marks them and no weather wears them. The binding never spent the convergence. It over-tuned the seam, and the amplification has held ever since, which is why geomancy and force magic spike here past anything a natural crossing should give.

The Circle has become a pilgrimage site for geomancers and force-attuned mages seeking to study or harness the immense power concentrated here. The draw and the danger are one fact. A working drawn on this over-tuned seam runs larger than it was aimed: Golus resolve sets the rock before the shaper means it to, and Dynus thrust lands at twice its intended size, because the seam answers harder here than anywhere. Pilgrims who reach past their own control are sometimes found set waist-deep into the ground, or flung clear of the ring.

The builders left no record the Northlanders can read. The working predates every people who might have named them, and the stones outlasted their makers' tongue. Whatever the ring was raised around, a central stone or a focus buried at its heart, still holds the charge of the moment the seam was bound, and that intact core is what keeps it over-tuned.

**The withheld answer (GM-facing; strips from public build).**

What the Circle is: the surviving stone armature of a deep-past Golus+Dynus working that bound the Earth+Force convergence. An early earth-and-force-attuned people raised the ring to seize the convergence's rare combined-working potential — the §6 compatible-pair amplification, Golus shaping and Dynus thrust fused into one durable working — and to hold a controlled tap on it.

The §9 cause is §6 seam-imprint / deformation, NOT a rupture and NOT a collapse. The builders bound the two leylines too well; the binding amplified the seam past stability, and when the working could no longer be held in check they could not safely release it. The ring did not explode (no scorched-plain rupture like the World Fire's #1 paragon over-channel) and did not slowly drift (unlike Firefrost's #3 geological drift). It LOCKED. The stones fused unbreakable in the moment the working set, and the convergence has answered over-large ever since: a seam imprinted/deformed into a permanent locked state (design bible §6, "a strong seam deforms local reality per its element"), distinct from the destabilization-collapse reserved for the Oblivion Years (#4).

Why the builders are gone: the binding outlasted them. Default reading — an early earth+force-attuned people who finished the binding and dispersed or died out in the deep past, leaving no tongue the Northlanders can read. They are NOT the Neferati (Fire, wrong region) and have NO tie to the Blight/Glassing cluster. The lead MAY later tie them to the wave-1 Earth race as a deep ancestor; that race's slug was not locked at authoring time, so the builders are left fully unnamed here (the §9.4 gap). Adding a named origin edge would resolve in public what this entry withholds — do not.

When: deep past, predating all Northlands civilizations and the World Fire (10 BSD). Kept undated in player prose — Lost Ages or earlier, for GM orientation only.

The players' lever (payoff): the central stone / buried focus still holds the working's charge — the thing keeping the convergence over-tuned. A party that finds and releases or re-binds it could calm the Circle (ending the danger to pilgrims) or unleash the stored convergence (an earth+force spillover). A defeatable, negotiable lever, not an inert mystery.
