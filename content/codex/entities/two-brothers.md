A distinctive pair of peaks north of Erebinthias, visible for miles along the approaches to Argysis. The Two Brothers mark the traditional boundary between Hephake and Argysis—or what was Argysis before the dragon came.

According to legend, the peaks were once a single mountain split in two during a duel between dwarven princes. The losing prince fell into the chasm between them; his bones lie there still. Whether this is history or myth, the Two Brothers have become a pilgrimage site for those mourning Argysis's fall. Dwarves climb to the col between the peaks to leave offerings and pray for their cousins' liberation.

The dragon Rothogomos knows of this tradition and finds it amusing. He occasionally sends his agents to observe the pilgrims, ensuring none attempt anything more active than prayer.
