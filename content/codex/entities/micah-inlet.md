Micah Inlet cuts into the confederation's eastern edge, where the land meets the coastal fringe nearest the Jungles of Godahi. Its sheltered waters make it the most reliable anchorage for coastal traders working the eastern approaches to the United Free Nations. Nominally administered by the nearest city-state, the inlet is treated in practice as shared confederation territory—a rare piece of common ground in a polity otherwise defined by city-state rivalry.
