**Domains:** Deep-current, battle-tide, the trident that strikes from the dark below.

**Era of ascension:** Great Expansion (~4,000 BSD). **Cohort: current.** Tromaran rose with the consolidation of triton martial culture. The warrior-cities of the deep shelf needed a patron of attack from cover, the maneuver their geography made natural and their neighbors made necessary.

**Worshipped by:** The tritons of the deep-shelf cities. Their warriors invoke him at the moment of breaking from cover; his name is the last word a triton speaks before the strike, and the first word said over a triton killed in one. (The worships edge is authored downstream on the worshipper, not here.)

Velaendor holds the sea as archive — the memory of what the surface forgot. Tromaran holds the same dark as a weapon. The two are not rivals; they are the two faces of the deep, and the triton and the kendor recognize this in each other across long oceanic distances. A triton war-band that surfaces to raid sings Tromaran's name into the swell as it leaves; a kendor coast hearing that song knows what is coming and reads it as Velaendor delivering a message in another daemon's voice.
