The largest state on Ve, Shyona occupies the continent's northeastern reach—a broad sweep of golden plains, dense forests, and sea-cliffs that stretches from the Turquish Bay coast in the north down to Meadow Sound, the great inlet where jungle first meets the sea in the south. The land draws its character from the dozens of close-packed towns scattered across the interior, their Japanese-flavored names a signature of a culture that values craft, ceremony, and the careful ordering of daily life.

## Geography

**Gilded Plains** anchor the central-northern interior. When the sun rises over the Sunrise Mountains to the east, the plains become a brilliant wash of gold—travelers say there is no more beautiful sight on Ve. The plains give way in every direction to forest: Sunigama and Hashigama crowd the northern edge, Tugachi and Hills of Gray Stone mark the western margins, and Bladewood and Bloodwood Forest press in from the south. The hills of Goataya and the Heights of Gray Stone break the otherwise flat interior with ragged ridges.

**Yokani Cliffs** form the entire eastern facade, dramatic sea-walls dropping to the waters below. East-facing settlements are sparse; most of Shyona's towns cluster in the protected interior valleys.

**Sunrise Mountains** rise in the northeast, dividing the Gilded Plains from the Yokani coast. They are not the tallest range on the continent, but their silhouette against the dawn sky gives them their name.

## Settlements

Shyona's interior is threaded with towns bearing the distinctive cadence of the local tongue: Kitsuregawa, Nashitsukuri, Higashi-kochi, Kunugatachi, Susakino, Tazawa, Ususuki, Ohama, and a score of others. None dominates the rest by size; Shyona's governance rests on a council of provincial lords, each answering to a high seat whose location has no formal capital in the graph. Shadowkeep, a ruin to the north, stands apart from this network entirely: its stone predates the current naming culture, and locals give it a wide berth.

The water margins hold quieter features—Einazawa, Fataku, Kugotori, Okarisumaba, Shurinato, and Tirawasun mark river mouths, coastal inlets, and sheltered bays used by fishing vessels.

## Borders

Shyona shares its southern edge with **Thorum**, the Widebarrow mountain state, and its western edge with **Edari**, the unclaimed savannah homeland of the nomadic Korel. Neither neighbor has formal agreements with Shyona's council lords; the frontier zones are lightly administered on all sides.
