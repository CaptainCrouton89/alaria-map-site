The major bay dominating Poum Titch's eastern coast, named for the silver-scaled whitefin fish that have sustained coastal communities for centuries. Whitefin is the primary protein source for most of the nation—smoked, salted, or fresh, it appears on every table.

The war has disrupted the fishing grounds. Naval skirmishes between loyalist and rebel vessels make open-water fishing dangerous. Catches are down. Prices are up. The coastal towns grow hungry, and hungry Scalawags grow desperate.
