King Ulyas has ruled Kerwin since before the Neferati founders came north. He negotiated Kerwin's entry into the empire five centuries ago, watched the Neferati throne dilute into humanity, witnessed the Severance, and has outlived every emperor since. He still rules the same halfling villages, manages the same modest tribute arrangements, and makes the same annual trip into the Fireknife Mountains with prisoners who do not return.

He carries everything the current dynasty would rather nobody remembered: the original bargains, the founding families' private histories, the real sequence of events during the Severance. He has not volunteered this knowledge. He has not decided whether he ever will.

Tarkhetan's officials have learned not to push Kerwin toward closer integration. The officials who tried had short careers. Ulyas remembers them too.
