The smallest lake, positioned between D'had and Naihim. Shisk is notable for its clarity—the stillwater effect means sediment never rises, and visibility extends to the bottom in most areas. Fish exist here but move in slow, languid patterns. They're easy to catch but taste faintly metallic.

Rumors persist of shapes visible in Shisk's depths—regular geometric forms that don't match natural formations. No one has successfully dived deep enough to investigate; the water's resistance makes descent exhausting and ascent terrifying.
