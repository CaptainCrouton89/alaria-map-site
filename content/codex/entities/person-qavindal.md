Qavindal was a human scholar of the invented-harsh tradition, writing in the Great Expansion, who spent decades in the ruins of the False God's administrative centers reconstructing how the Lost Ages' most powerful political entity actually governed. His work is narrow, obsessive, and invaluable: he is the only scholar who asked not what the False God was, but how its bureaucracy functioned — and what the physical record of that bureaucracy reveals about its end.
