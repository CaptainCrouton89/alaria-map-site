Mu sits on Nuin Bay in Sestros's southwest corner, where coastal trade routes meet the inland ranching economy of the Glory River valleys. A modest city by continental standards—perhaps 15,000 people—but the largest settlement in Sestros by a wide margin.

The royal palace dominates the skyline, a sprawling complex of whitewashed stone and silk banners that serves as both seat of government and primary temple to Talresses. The monarch here is no mere political office: Shailin doctrine holds that the ruler speaks directly for the god, which gives the palace's theological wing considerable authority over civil decisions. Priests and ministers share the same corridors, and the line between religious observance and governance is deliberately blurred.

The harbor handles most of Sestros's textile exports, loading bolts of the finest silk in Ve onto ships bound for Shyona, Chimea, and beyond. The textile workshops cluster near the docks, their dye-vats producing the brilliant colors that make Sestran silk unmistakable. Buyers come from across Ve; the harbor market during peak season is the most concentrated wealth in the kingdom outside the palace itself.
