In the western Eceraen foothills, where the mountains meet the coast, stands a lone marker that appears on some maps as Azkirk. The name is old—possibly pre-satyr—and its meaning is lost.

What Azkirk marks is unclear. Some maps show it as a settlement, others as a point of interest, still others as a warning. The location corresponds to a rocky headland where the mountain slopes plunge directly into the sea, creating a natural landmark visible from ships offshore.

Those who've investigated report finding nothing but stone—no structures, no artifacts, no obvious significance. Yet the name persists across centuries of cartography, copied from map to map by scribes who know only that something important once happened there, or will happen, or is happening still.

Local satyr avoid the area, claiming the ground is "marked." They don't explain what this means. The ulvsjael don't come this far south regardless. Whatever Azkirk represents, it remains a minor mystery in a region full of greater ones.
