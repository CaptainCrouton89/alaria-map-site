A region on Mount Kajiit's slopes where obsidian flows have created fields of volcanic glass. The Verucan harvest this obsidian for blades, ritual objects, and trade goods. Walking through Kedari Emnath is treacherous—the glass is sharp, the footing uncertain, and new flows can appear without warning.

The name translates roughly to "the black tears" in the Verucan tongue, referring to legends about the volcano weeping for those who died in the ancient eruption.
