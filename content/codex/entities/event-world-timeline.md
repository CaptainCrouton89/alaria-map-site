Time has consisted of three eons of full world cycles. Each cycle ends with a reality-warping event. The current cycle is still relatively young.

### The Three Eons

1. The Early Times — the first eon; Azus and Melera shape the cosmos, and the Age of Titans rises and falls within it; 4 billion–12 million years ago
2. The Gaeaic Eon — the second eon; Ezz floods Alaria, Gaea is born, and the eon closes with Hykravones the Shattering; 12 million–~13,500 years ago (10,209 BSD)
3. The Modern Era — the third and current eon; the rebuilding from the Shattering to the present day; ~13,500 years ago (10,209 BSD)–Now

Dating convention (reconciled 2026-06-03, calendar subsystem pass): the years-ago and BSD scales were double-counted at the Shattering. Fixed to: the Shattering = 10,210–10,209 BSD ≈ 13,500 years ago (NOT 10,209 years ago). A BSD year converts to years-before-present by adding the 3,376 years of the Seventh Dawn (Now = 3376 SD). The two scales meet inside the Lost Ages: deep-prehistory eras (Titans through the Age of Craggus) stay in round years-ago; the Lost Ages and everything after are dated in BSD/SD. See docs/worldbuilding/systems/calendar.md for the full convention and era date-range table. Hykravones is the terminal catastrophe of the Gaeaic Eon (within era-gaeaic-eon); the Modern Era (era-modern-era) opens at its close.
