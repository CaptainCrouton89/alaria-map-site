## Geography

Ve is a large, isolated continent to the far east of Alaria, separated from the main landmasses by vast ocean expanses. It stands apart—a world unto itself. The continent divides broadly into a drier northern half of savannah, plains, and desert, and a heavily jungled southern half dominated by wild beasts and ancient mysteries.

### Surrounding Waters

- **Alrock Ocean**: The vast ocean to the west, separating Ve from the rest of Alaria
- **Norswell Sea**: The northern waters
- **Sea of Fools**: The eastern ocean, what Meadow Sound opens to, lying due east of the Krell Lands. Despite its lovely appearance—filled with beautiful coral reefs and inviting waters—the Sea of Fools is treacherous. Every creature here, from the eels to the snails to the smallest reef dwellers, carries slow-acting poison. Not necessarily lethal, but awful nonetheless. The sea's beauty is a deadly lure, earning it the name "Sea of Fools" for those who trust what they see
- **Shadowreap**: Far to the east of Ve, north of the Sea of Fools, lies Shadowreap—a region where force and dark leylines intersect beneath the ocean. Massive clouds of pure darkness drift across the water, and sailing into them invites disaster. The combined power of force and darkness can tear ships apart, ripping hulls to splinters and scattering wreckage across the waves. Few vessels that enter Shadowreap return intact

### Continental Layout (Clockwise from Northwest)

**Northwest - Edari**: Savannah plains occupy the northwestern corner of Ve, a land of golden grasses and scattered trees.

**Shepherd's Stones**: Steep golden plains that form a natural barrier between Edari and Sestros, marking the transition between the two regions.

**North - Turquish Bay**: This bay separates Edari from Shyona, cutting into the northern coastline.

**Northeast - Shyona**: The largest state on Ve, occupying much of the northeastern region. Shyona is a Japan-inspired land of large plains (including the Gilded Plains), with dramatic **Yokani Cliffs** running along most of the eastern edge. The state extends south to **Meadow Sound**, a large inlet that penetrates deep into the continent where jungle meets the sea.

**East Coast - Wild Jungle**: South of Meadow Sound, the coast becomes untamed jungle.

**Southeast - Krell Lands**: The first major wild jungle region, occupying the eastern coastal jungle.

**South - Dygon Beast Lands**: A vast jungle stretching across the entire southern end of the continent from west to east. This is primordial wilderness, home to creatures found nowhere else.

**Southeast - Phyr Island**: A jungle island separated from the mainland by **Disaster Passage**, a treacherous strait.

**Southwest - Phriorys**: A region of hills between the Dygon Beast Lands and the Suki Jungle, home to the dragon **Pyaganos**.

**Southwest - Gulf of Chimea & Chimea**: The Gulf of Chimea indents the southwestern coast. The state of **Chimea** controls the **Suki Jungle** and extends through the Blood Mountains into the plains just south of Avalon. The Suki is divided into regions: South Suki and West Suki along the coasts, North Suki inland.

### Western Regions (North of the Mountains)

**Avalon**: Immediately north and west of the Widebarrow Mountains. A land of grasslands and forests, more temperate than the surrounding regions.

**Sestros**: North of Avalon. Transitions from plains in the south through savannah to desert in the north, including the **Red Desert**.

### North-Central Desert Region

Between Sestros and Shyona lie independent territories in a yellowy, arid landscape:

- **Hills of Dolor**: Hilly desert terrain with city-states
- **Dunes of Evioli**: Sandy desert with city-states

## Coquray Sound

The empty arm.

Coquray Sound is the western inlet of Turquish Bay, a long, narrow body of water that penetrates deep into the coastline west of Droughd Sound. Despite its sheltered waters and defensible position, Coquray Sound hosts no permanent settlements—an oddity that various explanations fail to fully account for.

### Geography

Coquray Sound stretches roughly twenty miles inland, narrower than Droughd Sound but deeper, its waters reaching sixty feet in places. The shoreline is rocky, with steep cliffs along the eastern edge and lower, marshy ground to the west where the sound approaches Sestros territory.

The sound's mouth opens to Turquish Bay between two prominent headlands. In theory, this creates an ideal harbor—defensible, sheltered from storms, with deep water for large vessels. In practice, no one has built there.

### Why It's Empty

Several explanations circulate:

**The Currents**: Coquray Sound has unusual water patterns. Currents flow unpredictably, changing direction without warning. Ships anchoring overnight sometimes find themselves dragged toward the cliffs despite secure moorings. Experienced sailors call the sound "treacherous" and avoid it when possible.

**The Depths**: Fishermen report the sound is deeper than soundings suggest—that instruments sometimes fail to find bottom where charts show forty feet of water. Whether this reflects actual depth variations or equipment malfunction remains unclear. Stories of things in the deep are common but unsubstantiated.

**The Claim**: Sestros technically claims Coquray Sound as part of its territorial waters, though they've never established presence there. Some believe Sestros deliberately keeps the sound empty as a potential naval staging area—somewhere ships could gather unobserved before operations elsewhere. If so, they've never used it for that purpose.

**The Practical**: Most likely, Coquray Sound is simply inconvenient. It leads nowhere valuable—no river connection, no inland resources worth accessing, no reason to build there when Droughd Sound serves perfectly well. Empty land stays empty when there's no profit in filling it.

### Current Use

Coquray Sound sees occasional traffic—ships seeking shelter from storms, smugglers looking for privacy, fishermen working waters their competitors avoid. None stay long. The sound has a reputation, deserved or not, and reputation shapes behavior.

Sestros naval vessels patrol the area irregularly, asserting the claim no one contests because no one cares enough to contest it. These patrols report nothing unusual. The sound remains empty, and no one seems motivated to change that.

## Marmoroy River

The largest northern tributary of the Misery River, the Marmoroy flows south from the highlands near Shyona's interior before joining the main channel east of Locquine. Its waters carry a milder version of the Misery's grief—still melancholic, but more wistful than crushing.

### Course

The Marmoroy rises in the forested hills that mark the transition between Shyona's settled farmland and the wild jungles of the south. It flows generally southwest, gathering smaller streams as it descends through increasingly dense vegetation. The river joins the Misery roughly twenty miles east of Locquine, their combined waters continuing toward Belanorn Bay.

The Marmoroy is navigable for small craft along much of its length, making it the preferred approach for traders seeking to reach the Misery River watershed from Shyona. The journey upstream is demanding but possible, unlike the Misery's main channel where the psychological effects intensify the closer one gets to the source.

### Character

The Marmoroy's waters produce melancholy rather than despair. Travelers report a sense of nostalgic sadness—memories of happier times, awareness of opportunities lost, gentle regret rather than crushing grief. Some find it almost pleasant, in a bittersweet way. Others compare it to the feeling of visiting a childhood home that has changed beyond recognition.

This milder effect likely results from distance. The Marmoroy doesn't flow from whatever contaminates the Misery's source. It merely joins the afflicted water partway through its course, picking up a diluted version of the grief before carrying it to the confluence.

### The Marmoroy Settlements

Several small communities line the Marmoroy's banks, particularly in its upper reaches where the effect is weakest. These villages serve as staging points for expeditions into Locquine territory and as trading posts for jungle products. The largest, **Sorrow's Rest**, sits at the navigable limit of the river—a cluster of perhaps fifty families who've made their peace with perpetual melancholy.

The villagers of the Marmoroy have developed customs around their environment. They celebrate the past rather than the future. They tell stories of the dead with fondness rather than grief. They've found that accepting the sadness rather than fighting it makes life in the watershed bearable.

### Trade Route

The Marmoroy provides the most practical water route into Locquine territory. Traders travel upstream by poled barge, spending several days on the river before transferring to smaller boats at Sorrow's Rest. From there, they can either continue up minor tributaries or travel overland to the Misery's main channel.

This route avoids the worst of the Misery's psychological effects while still providing water transport for bulky goods. The trade is modest but consistent—luxury items heading into Locquine, pixie artifacts heading out, and provisions moving in both directions.

### Why "Marmoroy"

The name comes from an old Shyonan word meaning "remembrance of stone." Early explorers noted that the river's rocky bed seemed to hold echoes of the past—travelers would find themselves remembering events that happened in this place before they arrived, as if the stones themselves retained memories. Whether this is a real phenomenon or simply the river's psychological effects manifesting as vivid imagination remains unclear.
