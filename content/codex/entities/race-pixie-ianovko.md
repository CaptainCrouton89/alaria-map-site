The Ianovko pixies are master illusionists who blur the line between dream and reality. They built their settlements in the spaces between — twilight groves where day meets night, misty valleys where earth meets sky, and in the minds of those who encountered them. An Ianovko community could exist simultaneously in the material plane and in the collective unconscious of a nearby village, feeding on dreams and nightmares alike.

Their wings appear to be made of captured starlight and shadow, constantly shifting between translucent and opaque. Ianovko culture prizes clever wordplay and elaborate pranks above all else, with social standing determined by the audacity and artistry of one's illusions.

The settlements are gone. Whatever obliterated them did so thoroughly enough that no account of it survived, which is itself a characteristic Ianovko outcome: a people who lived between layers of reality left no clear record of where those layers failed. What remains is a diaspora — Ianovko moving singly or in pairs through other communities, practicing their craft, and not speaking about what they lost. The pranks continue. The social accounting of audacity continues. The groves do not.
