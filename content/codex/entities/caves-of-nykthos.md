Deep in the eastern Aqueandrekons, carved into a mountainside that catches no direct sunlight, lie the Caves of Nykthos. These ancient ruins predate any known civilization in the region—their architecture suggests builders who found darkness comfortable and light painful.

The caves extend far into the mountain, their passages carved with geometric precision. The walls bear inscriptions in a language no living scholar has deciphered, and the deeper chambers contain what appear to be altars or focusing points for rituals long forgotten.

Something still dwells in the deepest reaches of Nykthos. Expeditions that venture too far report sounds of movement in the darkness, glimpses of shapes that don't match any known creature, and the unsettling sensation of being watched. Several expeditions have failed to return entirely. Those that do emerge often speak of dreams—vivid, recurring dreams of darkness and cold and something vast that waits.

The caves sit at no known leyline convergence, which makes their evident magical nature all the more disturbing. Whatever power lingers here, it predates the leyline system—or operates outside it entirely.
