A broad, sheltered bay or inland sea on Aal Salma's northern coast—warm, shallow, and rich with life. Pearl divers work its sandy bottoms. Fishing boats crisscross its calm waters. Trade ships move between Zamrat Aqabah and smaller coastal towns without risking the open ocean.

The bay's inlets and coves have always sheltered smugglers and, occasionally, pirates. Aal Salma's navy patrols when it can, but Masaad Takid has too many hiding places to control completely. The authorities mostly focus on keeping the main shipping lanes clear and let the margins police themselves.
