Argysis is a sub-range of the Kharvorn Mountains, site of a great Shyka dwarven kingdom until the black dragon Rothogomos seized it roughly two centuries ago. The dwarves did not submit. They fought — hold by hold, tunnel by tunnel — and were broken. Those who survived the conquest fled east across the Two Brothers and South Passage into Hephake; those who did not are bones beneath collapsed stone. Rothogomos now rules from the ruined capital of Othyndria, and no dwarven population remains.

#### The Dragon's Domain

Rothogomos took Argysis by betraying his brother Tepheranos, who rules Anarak to the south. The prize was the kingdom's accumulated treasury and the mountain defiles that command the northern Kharvorn. What Rothogomos won is a hoard buried inside an empty kingdom: its cities gutted, its forges cold, its deep roads caved in where the dwarves collapsed them during the retreat. The dragon governs nothing — there is no one to govern. He broods over his vaults, nurses his feud with Tepheranos, and keeps whatever lesser creatures find shelter in ruin useful as eyes and claws.

The holds and cities of old Argysis stand as the dwarves last left them: stripped of everything portable during the withdrawal, their gates sealed or shattered. Othyndria is the exception — Rothogomos tore it open to reach the treasury and has occupied the great hall ever since.

#### Pact of Barrik

Before the fall, the Pact of Barrik was a shared monument between Argysis and Hephake: a pair of dwarven kings carved back to back, one holding a battleaxe, the other a book, representing the martial and scholarly lines of the two kingdoms. Children of both realms visited the statue; princes spent days in meditation beside it. When Rothogomos took the mountains, safe passage to the statue ended and the tradition died. The statue still stands somewhere in contested Argysis — Hephake has not forgotten it.
