The Timekeeper city rises from a high plateau in the central Tethryns, its marble architecture stark white against the dark volcanic stone. See the full state entry for details on its astronomical traditions, the Great Clocktower, and the Chroniclers' Compact that guarantees its neutrality.

From a distance, Xi Chin appears to glow at dawn and dusk—the marble catching the light when the surrounding peaks have fallen into shadow. Pilgrims, students, and diplomats make the climb along the Wister Road, a switchback path carved into the mountainside over centuries. The journey takes three days from the coast, or two if you're young and foolish enough to push through the night.
