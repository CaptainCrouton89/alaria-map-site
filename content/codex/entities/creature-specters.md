A specter is what a soul or shadow becomes when necromancy strands it in the overlay it should have crossed. How the stranding happens cosmologically is covered in the entry on the specter as a process; the concern here is the thing left behind. Its spirit, the seat of will and self, fades to Celestia along its own strand and does not stay with the remnant. So what remains is a soul or shadow with no spirit to drive it. It has substance enough to act and nothing of its own to want.

That emptiness is what makes a specter useful. The necromancer who cut the thread holds the remnant, and a specter has nothing left in it to refuse.
